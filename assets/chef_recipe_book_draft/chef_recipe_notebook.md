### 1. Buttermilk Biscuits
**Page:** 1 | **Date noted:** 2-15-19 | **[CONFIDENCE: HIGH]**

#### Ingredients
- 2 cups AP flour
- 2 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp salt
- ¼ tsp baking soda
- 7 tbsp unsalted butter, chilled in freezer, cut into thin slices
- ¾ cup cold buttermilk
- 2 tbsp melted butter, for brushing

#### Technique
1. Whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, and baking soda.
2. Work chilled butter slices into the flour mixture until pea-sized — use your fingers or a bench scraper; work fast to keep butter cold.
3. Add cold buttermilk and mix just until the dough comes together. Do not overwork.
4. Turn out onto a floured surface, fold the dough 2–3 times to build layers.
5. Pat to about ¾-inch thickness. Cut with a floured biscuit cutter — press straight down, don't twist.
6. Place on a parchment-lined sheet pan. Brush tops with melted butter.
7. Bake at 425°F for 12–15 minutes until golden.

### 2. Vegan Biscuits
**Page:** 1 | **Date noted:** 2-15-19 | **[CONFIDENCE: HIGH]**

#### Ingredients
- 2 cups AP flour
- 2 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp salt
- 7 tbsp non-dairy butter (cold)
- ¾ cup cold almond milk
- 1 tbsp lemon juice or cider vinegar *(acts as vegan buttermilk)*
- 2 tbsp melted non-dairy butter, for brushing

#### Technique
1. Combine almond milk and lemon juice/cider vinegar; let sit 5 minutes to curdle into vegan "buttermilk."
2. Whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt.
3. Cut cold non-dairy butter into the flour mixture until pea-sized.
4. Add the vegan buttermilk and mix until just combined.
5. Fold, pat, cut, and bake exactly as buttermilk biscuits above — 425°F, 12–15 minutes.
6. Brush with melted non-dairy butter straight from the oven.

### 4. Orange Oleo
**Page:** 3 | **Date noted:** 5-4 | **[CONFIDENCE: HIGH]**

*"Oleo saccharum" — citrus oil extraction via sugar.*

#### Ingredients
- 50g orange peel
- 200g sugar

#### Method
1. Toss orange peels in the sugar. Coat well.
2. Let sit — allow sugar to draw out the essential oils (minimum 30 min; ideally 1–2 hrs).
3. Add 200 mL hot water.
4. Stir until sugar is fully dissolved.
5. Strain through fine-mesh strainer.

#### Notes
- Use only the zest/outer peel — avoid pith.
- Can be used as a cocktail modifier or syrup base.

### 5. Clarified OJ — Acidic
**Page:** 3 | **[CONFIDENCE: HIGH]**

#### Ingredients
- 650 mL orange juice
- 13g citric acid
- 6.5g malic acid
- 1.3g agar powder

#### Method
1. Combine acids with orange juice until dissolved.
2. Separate out ¼ of the juice mixture.
3. Add agar to the reserved ¼ portion.
4. Heat until boiling; stir 3 minutes.
5. Add remaining (unheated) juice into the hot agar mixture.
6. Pour into cheesecloth-lined container.
7. Freeze overnight.
8. Thaw in fridge over cheesecloth — the agar gel will trap the solids and the clarified juice drips through clean.

#### Notes
- This is a freeze-clarification / agar clarification hybrid technique.
- The agar captures the particulates; freezing and thawing collapses the gel and allows crystal-clear juice to drip through.

### 7. Jiban (was Jiban / Maza Cheese) (Pop-Up Prep)
**Page:** 4 | **Date noted:** 07-27 | **[CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM]** — clear technique notes; some words slightly ambiguous

*Note: "Maza" appears as the recipe name / menu label; "Jiban" is the Lebanese fresh cheese being made. Pop-up context noted.*

#### Ingredients
- 3 gallons whole milk
- 1½ cups whipping cream
- 1 rennet tablet
- Muslin / cheesecloth

#### Method
1. Heat milk to 70–80°F. **Do not boil.**
2. Dissolve rennet tablet in a little cold water; stir into milk.
3. Stir into milk on low temp until milk coagulates — keep on low heat and stir gently.
4. Once fully coagulated, scoop curds onto muslin; let strain.
5. Wrap drained cheese into parcels; form into squares.
6. Wrap in muslin and place on a cookie sheet in the fridge.
7. Let drain overnight in fridge.

#### Notes
- Whipping cream addition gives a richer, creamier curd than whole milk alone.
- Do not let milk get too hot after adding rennet — heat denatures the rennet enzyme.
- Final cheese is a fresh, soft Lebanese-style white cheese — mild, milky, lightly salted.
- Salt to taste after draining; optionally brine for storage.

### 9. Hummus
**Page:** 6 | **[CONFIDENCE: HIGH]**

#### Ingredients
- 1 can chickpeas
- 4 cloves garlic
- 3 tbsp tahini
- 1 tbsp EVOO
- 1 tsp salt
- Juice of 2 / zest of 1 lemon

#### Technique [TECHNIQUE ADDED — quantities confirmed from scan, method standard]
1. Drain and rinse chickpeas. Reserve liquid (aquafaba) for thinning.
2. Add chickpeas, garlic, tahini, lemon juice, lemon zest, and salt to a food processor.

### 10. Labneh (was Haydari / Laban)
**Page:** 6 | **[CONFIDENCE: LOW — title only; no ingredients or method recorded]**
**[SOURCE: INFERRED]** — Recipe reconstructed from standard Turkish meze haydari

*"Haydari" is a thick Turkish yogurt dip; "Laban" suggests a strained yogurt / labneh component. Likely intended as a meze pair or the base for haydari.*

#### Ingredients
- 500g full-fat Greek yogurt (strained overnight through cheesecloth for extra thickness — or use labneh)
- 40g feta cheese, crumbled
- 2 cloves garlic, finely grated or mashed to a paste
- ¼ cup fresh mint, finely chopped
- ¼ cup fresh dill, finely chopped
- ½ tsp dried mint
- ½ tsp salt
- 1 tbsp EVOO (plus more for finishing)
- Optional: pinch of Aleppo pepper / pul biber for garnish

#### Method
1. If straining your own yogurt: line a strainer with cheesecloth, add yogurt, place in fridge 12–24 hours until very thick.
2. In a bowl, mash feta with a fork until mostly smooth.
3. Add strained yogurt, garlic paste, fresh mint, fresh dill, dried mint, and salt. Mix well.
4. Cover and chill at least 30 minutes before serving (longer = better garlic infusion).
5. Serve drizzled with EVOO, garnish with dill and Aleppo pepper if desired.

#### Notes
- Haydari is thicker than tzatziki and contains no cucumber.
- Garlic flavor intensifies as it sits — make day-of or adjust garlic to taste.

### 11. Yufka
**Page:** 7 | **[CONFIDENCE: LOW — title only]**
**[SOURCE: INFERRED]** — Turkish thin flatbread / pastry dough

*Yufka is Turkish unleavened flatbread, similar to lavash, used as a meze bread or dough for börek-style preparations.*

#### Ingredients
- 3 cups (375g) AP flour
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 cup warm water (approximately — add gradually)
- 1 tbsp oil (optional)

#### Method
1. Combine flour and salt; add warm water gradually and mix until a smooth, pliable dough forms. Rest covered 30 minutes.
2. Divide into golf ball-sized portions. Roll each very thin on a lightly floured surface — nearly translucent.
3. Cook on a dry hot cast-iron or griddle, 30–60 seconds per side, until lightly speckled and cooked through but still pliable.
4. Stack under a damp towel to keep soft.

#### Notes
- Thicker than phyllo, thinner than pita — more like lavash.
- For börek / pastry use, keep uncooked and layer with filling.

### 12. Pickling Base
**Page:** 7 | **[CONFIDENCE: LOW — title only]**
**[SOURCE: INFERRED]** — Standard quick pickling brine

#### Ingredients
- 1 cup white wine vinegar (or rice wine vinegar)
- 1 cup water
- 1 tbsp kosher salt
- 1 tbsp sugar
- Optional aromatics: peppercorns, bay leaf, garlic, mustard seed, red pepper flakes

#### Method
1. Combine vinegar, water, salt, and sugar in a saucepan.
2. Bring to a simmer over medium heat; stir until salt and sugar dissolve completely.
3. Add any aromatics.
4. Pour hot brine over prepared vegetables in sterilized jars.
5. Cool to room temperature, then refrigerate. Ready in 1–2 hours; best after 24 hours.

#### Notes
- Adjust vinegar-to-water ratio for more or less acidity (1:1 is balanced; 2:1 is sharp).
- Can be used warm or cold depending on application.

### 13. Baba Ghanoush (was Baba Ghanouj)
**Page:** 8 | **[CONFIDENCE: LOW — title only]**
**[SOURCE: INFERRED]** — Classic Lebanese/levantine version

#### Ingredients
- 2 large eggplants
- 2–3 cloves garlic
- 3 tbsp tahini
- Juice of 1–2 lemons
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp EVOO (plus more for finishing)
- Optional: ½ tsp smoked paprika, chopped parsley for garnish

#### Method
1. Char eggplants directly over an open flame (or under broiler), turning occasionally, until skin is blackened and flesh is completely soft — about 15–20 minutes.
2. Let cool, then peel and discard charred skin. Drain excess liquid.
3. Roughly chop the flesh; place in a bowl.
4. Mash in garlic (use a mortar or grate on a microplane), then fold in tahini, lemon juice, and salt.
5. Taste and adjust — it should be smoky, tangy, and creamy.
6. Plate and finish with a generous pour of EVOO. Garnish with paprika and parsley.

#### Notes
- The char is everything — don't rush it. Open flame gives the most authentic smoky flavor.

### 14. Second Courses — Tabbouleh
**Page:** 8 | **[CONFIDENCE: LOW — title only]**
**[SOURCE: INFERRED]** — Classic Lebanese tabbouleh (herb-forward, not bulgur-heavy)

#### Ingredients
- 2 bunches flat-leaf parsley, very finely chopped
- ½ bunch fresh mint, finely chopped
- 2 medium tomatoes, very small dice
- 4–5 green onions / scallions, finely sliced
- ¼ cup fine bulgur wheat
- Juice of 2–3 lemons
- ¼ cup EVOO
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp allspice (optional)

#### Method
1. Soak bulgur in cold water 10 minutes; drain and squeeze dry.
2. Combine parsley, mint, tomato, and green onion.
3. Add bulgur, lemon juice, EVOO, salt, and allspice.
4. Toss well. Taste — it should be very lemony and herb-forward.
5. Rest 15–20 minutes before serving so bulgur absorbs the juices.

#### Notes
- Authentic Lebanese tabbouleh is mostly parsley; bulgur is the minority.
- Fine bulgur (#1) is traditional — don't use coarse.

### 15. Rooz M3 Ful / Ful
**Page:** 9 | **[CONFIDENCE: LOW — title only]**
**[SOURCE: INFERRED]**

#### Ful Medames (Stewed Fava Beans)
**Ingredients:**
- 2 cans (or 400g dried, soaked overnight) fava beans
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- Juice of 2 lemons
- 3 tbsp EVOO
- 1 tsp cumin
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

#### Notes
- Ful Medames is traditionally served for breakfast in Lebanon and Egypt.
- "Rooz M3 Ful" would combine this with rice, cooked separately with vermicelli noodles in butter (Lebanese rice style).

### 16. Kibbeh Ni'yee (was Roa Kibbee Ni'yee)
**Page:** 9 | **[CONFIDENCE: LOW — title only]**
**[SOURCE: INFERRED]**

*"Kibbeh Ni'yee" (was "Roa Kibbee Ni'yee") = Raw Kibbeh (Kibbeh Nayyeh) — Lebanese raw lamb preparation, levantine steak tartare equivalent.*

#### Ingredients
- 500g very fresh, very lean lamb (leg or shoulder), sinew removed — ground fine or minced by hand
- 1 cup fine bulgur (#1 or #2 fine)
- 1 small onion, very finely grated
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp allspice
- ½ tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of cumin
- Pinch of cayenne
- Ice water as needed
- EVOO and fresh mint to finish

#### Method
1. Soak bulgur in cold water 15–20 minutes; drain and squeeze completely dry.
2. Combine ground lamb, bulgur, grated onion, and spices.
3. Knead mixture together, adding ice water a tbsp at a time to keep it smooth and cold. The mixture should be silky and homogeneous.
4. Taste for seasoning — adjust salt and spice.
5. Shape into a flat oval on a plate. Score the surface with a fork or knife.
6. Drizzle generously with EVOO. Garnish with mint.
7. Serve immediately with flatbread, scallions, and radishes.

#### ⚠️ Safety Note
- Use only fresh, high-quality lamb from a trusted source. Serve the day of preparation. Keep refrigerated until service.

### 17. Mih-Sheh / Enis & Lah'a [?]
**Page:** 10 | **[CONFIDENCE: LOW — title only; partial transliteration]**
**[SOURCE: INFERRED]**

#### Rice & Meat Filling (Hashweh)
**Ingredients:**
- 250g ground lamb or beef
- 1 cup short-grain rice, rinsed
- 1 tsp allspice
- ½ tsp cinnamon
- ½ tsp black pepper
- ½ tsp anise seed (fresh reading: "enis")
- Salt to taste
- 2 tbsp butter or oil

- Zucchini (cored), grape leaves, small eggplant, bell peppers — per batch

**Method:**
1. Brown ground meat in butter. Season with allspice, cinnamon, pepper, anise, and salt.

4. Layer stuffed vegetables in a heavy-bottomed pot; add enough salted water or broth to cover.
5. Place a plate on top as a weight. Cover and simmer 30–40 minutes until rice is cooked through.
6. Invert to serve or plate individually. Squeeze lemon over top.

### 18. Hind-Seh Mi'lia [?]

*"Hind-Seh Mi'lia" = possibly "Hindbeh Ma'lia" or similar — Lebanese sautéed bitter greens (dandelion greens / chicory) with caramelized onions. Common mezze.*

#### Ingredients

- ¼ cup EVOO
- Juice of 1 lemon

### 19. Dolma
**Page:** 11 | **[CONFIDENCE: LOW — title only]**
**[SOURCE: INFERRED]**

*Turkish/Levantine stuffed grape leaves.*

#### Ingredients
- 1 jar (or fresh) grape leaves, rinsed
- 1 cup short-grain rice, rinsed
- ½ lb ground lamb or beef (or omit for vegetarian)
- 1 onion, finely diced
- ¼ cup fresh dill, chopped
- ¼ cup fresh mint, chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- Juice of 2 lemons
- ¼ cup EVOO
- 1 tsp allspice
- ½ tsp cinnamon
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 cups water or broth for cooking

#### Method
1. Combine rice, meat (if using), onion, herbs, spices, half the lemon juice, and EVOO. Mix well.
2. Lay a grape leaf vein-side up. Place a small amount of filling near the stem. Fold in sides, then roll up tightly.
3. Layer dolma seam-side down in a heavy pot lined with extra grape leaves or sliced tomatoes.
4. Pour remaining lemon juice and broth/water over dolma. Cover with a heavy plate.
5. Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook on very low heat 45–60 minutes until rice is tender.
6. Let rest 10 minutes before serving. Serve with yogurt or lemon wedges.

### 24. Budino
**Page:** 15 | **[CONFIDENCE: HIGH]**

*Brown sugar caramel custard — Italian butterscotch pudding style.*

#### Ingredients
- 1 cup + 2 tbsp brown sugar
- ½ cup water
- 1½ tsp salt
- 3 cups heavy cream
- 1½ cups milk
- 1 whole egg
- 3 egg yolks
- 6 tbsp cornstarch
- 5 tbsp butter
- 1½ tbsp dark rum

#### Method
1. **Make the caramel:** Combine brown sugar, water, and salt in a heavy saucepan. Cook over medium-high without stirring until it reaches a deep amber caramel.
2. **Seize with milk + cream:** Carefully add cream and milk to the caramel. Whisk — it will bubble. Return to heat and stir to dissolve any hardened bits.
