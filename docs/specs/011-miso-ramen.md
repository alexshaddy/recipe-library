## Design

### 1. Ingredients
Grouped by component: broth, tare (seasoning base), noodles, toppings.

**For the Broth (makes about 6 cups):**
- 4 cups (960ml) chicken stock (low-sodium)
- 2 cups (480ml) water
- 1 piece (4x6 inch) kombu
- 1/2 cup (15g) katsuobushi (bonito flakes)
- 1 lb (450g) chicken bones (backs, necks) or pork bones (optional for richer broth)
- 1 onion, halved
- 1 carrot, chopped
- 1 stalk celery, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 thumb ginger, sliced
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar

**For the Miso Tare (seasoning base):**
- 3 tbsp white miso (shiro miso)
- 2 tbsp red miso (aka miso)
- 2 tbsp mirin
- 1 tbsp sake
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated

**For the Noodles:**
- 12 oz (340g) fresh or dried ramen noodles (alkaline wheat noodles)
- Water for boiling

**For Toppings (per bowl):**
- 2-3 slices chashu (braised pork belly) or grilled chicken thigh
- 1/2 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen, blanched)
- 1 tbsp butter
- 1/2 cup bean sprouts, blanched
- 2 stalks scallions (green onions), sliced
- 2 sheets nori (seaweed), torn
- 1 soft-boiled egg (ajitsuke tamago), halved
- Optional: sesame seeds, chili oil, black pepper

### 2. Instructions
Write 7–9 steps with bolded action titles, sensory cues, inline timing.

1. **Make the Broth** – In a large pot, combine chicken stock, water, kombu, chicken/pork bones (if using), onion, carrot, celery, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and sugar. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Do not boil; maintain a low simmer (tiny bubbles at edges) for 30–40 minutes. Remove kombu after 20 minutes to avoid sliminess. Add katsuobushi in the last 5 minutes of simmering, then turn off heat and let steep 10 minutes. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve lined with cheesecloth. You should have about 6 cups clear broth.
2. **Prepare the Tare** – While broth simmers, combine white miso, red miso, mirin, sake, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger in a small bowl. Mix until smooth paste forms a tablespoon of hot broth into the miso mixture to thin, then stir back into the bowl. Set aside.
3. **Cook the Noodles** – Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add ramen noodles and cook according to package directions (usually 2–3 minutes for fresh, 4–5 minutes for dried) until just al dente. Drain and rinse briefly under warm water to prevent sticking. Divide noodles among serving bowls.
4. **Prepare Toppings** – While noodles cook, warm chashu slices in microwave or pan. Blanch bean sprouts 30 seconds in hot water, drain. Slice scallions. If using, warm corn and butter in a small pan. Prepare soft-boiled eggs (boil 6–7 minutes, cool in ice water, peel, halve).
5. **Combine Broth and Tare** – Return strained broth to pot and keep warm over low heat. Ladle about 2 tablespoons of tare into each serving bowl (adjust to taste). Add 1 cup hot broth to each bowl and whisk to dissolve tare completely.
6. **Assemble the Bowl** – Pour the remaining hot broth (about 1 cup per bowl) into each bowl over the noodles. The noodles should be submerged.
7. **Add Toppings** – Arrange chashu, corn with butter, bean sprouts, scallions, nori, and egg halves on top of the noodles. Sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired.
8. **Serve Immediately** – Encourage diners to mix toppings into the broth and noodles before eating. Enjoy while hot.
9. **Optional: Adjust Seasoning** – Provide extra tare, soy sauce, or chili oil at the table for customization.

### 3. Notes & Variations
- **Cook's Notes (2):**
  1. The tare (misobase) is concentrated; dilute it with hot broth before adding to the bowl to ensure even distribution and prevent overly salty spots.
  2. For a richer broth, roast the bones and vegetables at 400°F (200°C) for 30 minutes before simmering.
- **Make-Ahead / Storage:**
  - Broth (without tare) can be made ahead and refrigerated for up to 3 days or frozen for 1 month.
  - Tare keeps refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 1 week.
  - Chashu and soft-boiled eggs can be prepared 2 days ahead and stored separately.
  - Noodles are best cooked fresh; if needed, fresh noodles can be par-cooked, oiled, and refrigerated for up to 1 day.
- **Scaling:**
  - The recipe scales linearly; maintain the ratio of tare to broth (about 2 tbsp tare per cup of broth).
  - For larger batches, use a larger stockpot and adjust simmering time slightly.
- **Variations (2):**
  - **Vegetarian Miso Ramen:** Use kombu-shiiitake dashi (kombu + dried shiitake) instead of chicken/pork broth; replace chashu with grilled tofu or tempeh.
  - **Spicy Miso Ramen:** Add 1 tablespoon chili paste (such as doubanjiang or sambal oelek) to the tare, or serve with chili oil on the side.
