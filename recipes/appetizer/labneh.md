# Labneh

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Best-effort mezze reconstruction from the page 6 title only.

## Ingredients

_Base servings: 1 batch_

- [ ] `2 cups` — thick yogurt
- [ ] `1 clove` — garlic, grated
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — olive oil
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — chopped mint or dill
- [ ] `salt` — to taste
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — lemon juice

## Instructions

1. **Strain the yogurt.** Line a sieve with cheesecloth and pour in the yogurt. Gather the cloth and tie it closed. Suspend over a bowl or set in the sieve, and let drain in the refrigerator overnight (about 12 hours) until thick and spreadable.
2. **Discard the whey.** Discard the liquid that has drained out.
3. **Whip and season.** Once set, whip the labneh until smooth. Stir in the grated garlic, salt to taste, and lemon juice. Adjust to taste.
4. **Finish and serve.** Transfer to a serving bowl, drizzle with olive oil, and garnish with chopped mint or dill.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes

- The notebook gives only the title, so this is a traditional-style reconstruction rather than a literal transcription.
- Longer straining yields a thicker, cream-cheese-like labneh; shorter yields a softer, scoopable dip.

### Make-Ahead / Storage

- Best after a short chill; use within a few days.

### Scaling

- Keep it thick and spoonable rather than loose.
