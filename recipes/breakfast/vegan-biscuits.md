# Vegan Biscuits

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Straight transcription from notebook page 1.

## Ingredients

_Base servings: 8 biscuits_

- [ ] `2 cups` — all-purpose flour
- [ ] `2 teaspoons` — baking powder
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — salt
- [ ] `7 tablespoons` — non-dairy butter, cold
- [ ] `3/4 cup` — almond milk, cold
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — lemon juice or cider vinegar


## Instructions

1. **Make the vegan buttermilk.** Stir the almond milk and lemon juice together and let it sit for 5 minutes until slightly curdled and fragrant.
2. **Mix the dry ingredients.** Whisk the flour, baking powder, and salt together for 30 seconds, until the mixture is uniformly speckled.
3. **Cut in the butter.** Add the cold non‑dairy butter and use a pastry cutter to work it into the flour until the pieces are pea‑sized and the mixture feels sandy, about 2 minutes.
4. **Combine wet and dry.** Pour the curdled milk into the flour mixture and stir gently for 1 minute, just until a shaggy dough forms.
5. **Fold for layers.** Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface, pat into a rectangle, then fold it over itself 2–3 times; this creates lift and should take about 1 minute.
6. **Shape and chill.** Pat the dough to about 3/4‑inch thickness, cut biscuits with a cutter, and chill on the sheet‑pan for 5 minutes while the oven preheats.
7. **Brush and bake.** Brush tops with a little melted vegan butter, then bake at 425 °F for 12–15 minutes, until the biscuits are golden‑brown and emit a nutty aroma.
8. **Cool and serve.** Remove from oven, let cool on a rack for 5 minutes; the biscuits should feel light and fluffy when broken open.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes

- The structure is the same as the dairy version; only the fat and liquid change, so keep the butter cold to maintain layers.
- Over‑mixing will develop gluten and make the biscuits tough; stop mixing as soon as the dough comes together.
- For a richer flavor, brush the tops with melted vegan butter mixed with a pinch of maple syrup before baking.

### Variations

- **Herb‑Infused Biscuits:** Add 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme or rosemary to the dry ingredients for an aromatic twist.
- **Whole‑Grain Version:** Substitute 1 cup of whole‑wheat flour for all‑purpose flour; increase the almond milk by 2 tablespoons to maintain moisture.

### Make‑Ahead / Storage

- Best served warm from the oven, but they can be reheated in a low oven for 8 minutes. Store cooled biscuits in an airtight container for up to 2 days.

### Scaling

- Keep the ratio of flour to fat the same if you scale. For larger batches, chill the dough before cutting to prevent spreading.
