# Aioli

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Best-effort reconstruction from the notebook addendum and page 5 of the scan.

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 1 batch*

- [ ] `4 cups` — neutral oil
- [ ] `2 tablespoons` — white wine vinegar
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — extra-virgin olive oil
- [ ] `5 cloves` — garlic
- [ ] `1` — large egg
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — parsley, chopped
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — fennel seed, crushed
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — salt
- [ ] `1/2 cup` — lemon juice
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — lemon zest

## Instructions

1. **Build the base.** Put everything except the oil into a blender and process until the garlic is broken down and the mixture looks loose and pale.
2. **Emulsify slowly.** Stream in the oil in a thin ribbon while the blender runs; the sauce should thicken and turn glossy.
3. **Adjust the balance.** Taste for salt, acid, and garlic intensity, then thin with a little water if the aioli feels too dense.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- This reads like a garlic-lemon aioli with fennel. The oil needs to go in slowly or the emulsion will split.

### Variations
- **Sharper version:** Add a little more vinegar if you want it brighter and less rich.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- Refrigerate tightly covered and use within 3 days.

### Scaling
- Keep the acid and egg proportional if you scale the batch.
