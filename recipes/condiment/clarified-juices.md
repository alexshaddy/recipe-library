# Clarified Juices

> [!WARNING]
> The notebook contains two related approaches on page 3: an acidic clarified orange juice and an agar clarification variant.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 1 batch*

- [ ] `650 milliliters` — orange juice
- [ ] `13 grams` — citric acid
- [ ] `6.5 grams` — malic acid
- [ ] `1.3 grams` — agar powder

---

## Instructions

1. **Combine the acids.** Stir the citric and malic acid into the orange juice until dissolved.
2. **Reserve part of the batch.** Pull off a quarter of the juice mixture and combine it with the agar.
3. **Boil the agar portion.** Bring the reserved portion to a boil and hold it there briefly so the agar fully activates.
4. **Finish the clarification.** Stir in the remaining juice, pour into a cheesecloth-lined container, freeze overnight, then thaw in the refrigerator so the clarified juice drips through.

---

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- This is a freeze-clarification / agar hybrid, which makes sense for a bar prep component.

### Variations
- **Alternative juices:** Use the same technique on other fruit juices once the ratio is confirmed.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- Refrigerate covered and use as a cocktail component within a few days.

### Scaling
- Keep the agar ratio proportional if you scale the batch.
