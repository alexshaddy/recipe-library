# Ricotta

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Best-effort transcription from notebook page 24.

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 1 batch*

- [ ] `½ gallon (2 quarts)` — milk
- [ ] `½ cup` — lemon juice (freshly squeezed)
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — salt

## Instructions

1. **Heat the Milk** – Pour milk into a heavy-bottomed pot. Warm over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until steaming and small bubbles form around the edges (about 180°F/82°C), 8–10 minutes. Do not boil.
2. **Add Acid** – Remove pot from heat. Stir in lemon juice gently; the milk will immediately begin to curdle, forming white curds floating in greenish whey. Let sit undisturbed for 5 minutes.
3. **Salt the Curds** – Sprinkle salt over the curds and stir gently to distribute.
4. **Line the Strainer** – Set a fine-mesh sieve over a bowl and line with cheesecloth or a clean cotton towel.
5. **Transfer Curds** – Using a slotted spoon, transfer the curds into the lined sieve, letting excess whey drain back into the bowl.
6. **Drain to Desired Consistency** – Gather the cheesecloth corners and twist gently to press out more whey. For softer ricotta, drain 10–15 minutes; for firmer, drain up to 1 hour, occasionally twisting.
7. **Use or Store** – Transfer ricotta to a container. Use immediately or refrigerate.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- The notebook almost certainly records a fresh-cheese method, even if the exact acid is not preserved cleanly.
- Using fresh lemon juice yields a cleaner, brighter acidity than bottled lemon juice or vinegar; if using vinegar, choose a mild white wine or apple cider variety.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- Transfer ricotta to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 5 days. For longer storage, freeze in portions for up to 2 months; thaw in the refrigerator before use.
- If whey separates during storage, simply stir it back in or drain off excess for a thicker texture.

### Scaling
- The recipe scales linearly: maintain the ratio of 4 parts milk to 1 part acid by volume (e.g., 1 quart milk + ¼ cup acid yields about ½ cup ricotta). Adjust salt proportionally.
- For larger batches, use a larger pot and ensure even heating to prevent scorching.

### Variations
- **Herbed Ricotta:** Stir in 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh herbs (such as basil, chives, or parsley) and a pinch of black pepper after draining.
- **Sweet Ricotta:** Mix in 1–2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup and a pinch of cinnamon for a dessert spread or filling.
