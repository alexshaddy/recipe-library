# Chiliburgers

> [!NOTE] Origin
> From a Carnation evaporated milk pamphlet headed "5 Ground Beef Ideas." This recipe uses the shared beef mixture from the page.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 8 burgers*

### Beef Mixture

- [ ] `2/3 cup` — undiluted evaporated milk
- [ ] `1 1/2 pounds` — ground chuck
- [ ] `1/2 cup` — fine cracker meal
- [ ] `1` — egg
- [ ] `1/2 cup` — onion, chopped
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — prepared mustard
- [ ] `1 1/2 teaspoons` — salt
- [ ] `1/8 teaspoon` — black pepper

### Chili Topping

- [ ] `1/4 cup` — chili sauce
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — prepared mustard
- [ ] `1/2 to 1 teaspoon` — chili powder

### For Serving

- [ ] `8` — split hamburger buns, toasted

---

## Instructions

1. **Make the beef mixture.** Combine the evaporated milk, ground chuck, cracker meal, egg, onion, mustard, salt, and pepper until just mixed and evenly combined.
2. **Form the patties.** Shape the mixture into 8 patties and place them on a broiler pan.
3. **Broil the burgers.** Broil about 5 inches from the heat for roughly 6 minutes per side, until browned and cooked through with a juicy center.
4. **Glaze with chili sauce.** During the last 5 minutes, mix the chili sauce, mustard, and chili powder, then spread it over the burgers so it bubbles lightly.
5. **Serve immediately.** Slide the chiliburgers onto toasted buns and serve hot.

---

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- The chili-sauce glaze goes on near the end so it stays glossy instead of scorching.

### Variations
- **Classic burger version:** Skip the chili topping and serve the patties plain.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- Shape the patties ahead and chill them covered for up to 1 day.
- Cooked burgers are best served immediately; reheat gently to avoid drying them out.

### Scaling
- The beef mixture is straightforward to scale up or down as long as the seasoning stays balanced.
