# Chiliburgers

> [!NOTE] Origin
> From a Carnation evaporated milk pamphlet headed "5 Ground Beef Ideas." This recipe uses the shared beef mixture from the page.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 8 burgers*

### Beef Mixture

- [ ] `2/3 cup` — undiluted evaporated milk
- [ ] `1 1/2 pounds` — ground chuck
- [ ] `1/2 cup` — fine cracker meal
- [ ] `1` — egg
- [ ] `1/2 cup` — onion, chopped
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — prepared mustard
- [ ] `1 1/2 teaspoons` — salt
- [ ] `1/8 teaspoon` — black pepper

### Chili Topping

- [ ] `1/4 cup` — chili sauce
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — prepared mustard
- [ ] `1/2 to 1 teaspoon` — chili powder

### For Serving

- [ ] `8` — split hamburger buns, toasted

---

## Instructions

1. **Make the Beef Mixture.** Combine the evaporated milk, ground chuck, cracker meal, egg, onion, mustard, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Mix lightly with your hands or a fork until just evenly combined (~1 min) — overmixing makes tough burgers.

2. **Form the Patties.** Shape the mixture into 8 patties of equal size, about 3/4 inch thick. Place them on a broiler pan, leaving space between each for even cooking.

3. **Broil the Burgers.** Broil about 5 inches from the heat until the tops are browned and sizzling (~6 min per side). The patties should be cooked through with a juicy center and a lightly charred exterior.

4. **Glaze with Chili Sauce.** During the last 5 minutes of cooking, mix the chili sauce, mustard, and chili powder in a small bowl. Spread the glaze over the burgers and return to the broiler — the glaze will bubble and turn glossy as it caramelizes.

5. **Serve Immediately.** Slide the chiliburgers onto toasted buns and serve hot while the glaze is still shiny and the patties are juicy.

---

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- The chili-sauce glaze goes on near the end so it stays glossy instead of scorching.
- The evaporated milk in the beef mixture adds moisture and richness, keeping the patties juicy even when cooked to well-done — a classic mid-century trick.

### Variations
- **Classic burger version:** Skip the chili topping and serve the patties plain.

### Make-Ahead
- Shape the patties ahead and chill them covered for up to 1 day.

### Storage
- Cooked burgers are best served immediately; reheat gently to avoid drying them out. Leftovers keep refrigerated for up to 2 days.

### Scaling
- The beef mixture is straightforward to scale up or down as long as the seasoning stays balanced.
