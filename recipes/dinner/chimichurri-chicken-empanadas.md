# Chimichurri Chicken Empanadas

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Expanded from Chef's Recipe Notebook page 19 (6.21 pop-up dinner notebook). Three-component recipe: chimichurri (exact weights from notebook), chicken filling with aromatics, and empanada dough (water resolved to 60% hydration). Can be baked (healthier, even browning) or fried (traditional, shatter crisp).

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 12 empanadas (3–4 per person as main)*

### Chimichurri (makes ~1 cup; use ½ cup for filling, reserve ½ cup for serving)
- [ ] `100 g` (≈2 cups packed) **fresh cilantro**, stems and leaves, roughly chopped
- [ ] `100 g` (≈2 cups packed) **flat-leaf parsley**, stems and leaves, roughly chopped
- [ ] `6` cloves **garlic** (≈18 g), minced
- [ ] `1] `1 tsp` **red pepper flakes** (adjust to heat preference)
- [ ] `1 tsp` **fine sea salt**
- [ ] `100 g` (½ cup) **red wine vinegar** (5% acidity)
- [ ] `200 g` (⅞ cup) **extra-virgin olive oil** (fruity, not bitter)
- [ ] `1 tbsp` **fresh oregano leaves** (optional, traditional in some regions)

### Chicken Filling
- [ ] `2 tbsp` **extra-virgin olive oil**
- [ ] `1` medium **yellow onion** (≈150 g), finely diced (¼-inch)
- [ ] `1` **red bell pepper** (≈150 g), finely diced (¼-inch)
- [ ] `3 cloves` **garlic**, minced
- [ ] `1 tsp` **ground cumin**
- [ ] `1 tsp` **smoked paprika (pimentón)**
- [ ] `½ tsp` **dried oregano**
- [ ] `¼ tsp` **ground cinnamon** (traditional Argentine touch)
- [ ] `2 cups` (≈300 g) **cooked chicken**, hand-shredded (poached, roasted, or rotisserie)
- [ ] `½ cup` (120 ml) **chimichurri** (from above, drained of excess oil — see Cook's Notes)
- [ ] `¼ cup` **pitted green olives**, roughly chopped (optional but traditional)
- [ ] `2 tbsp` **raisins** or **golden sultanas** (optional, sweet counterpoint)
- [ ] `1` **hard-boiled egg**, diced (optional, traditional *empanada salteña* style)
- [ ] `1 tsp` **fine sea salt**, plus more to taste
- [ ] `¼ tsp` **freshly ground black pepper**

### Empanada Dough (Masa para Empanadas)
- [ ] `500 g` (4 cups) **all-purpose flour**, plus more for dusting
- [ ] `10 g` (2 tsp) **fine sea salt**
- [ ] `1 tsp` **baking powder** (for tender crumb)
- [ ] `100 g` (7 tbsp) **unsalted butter**, cold and cubed (or 50g butter + 50g lard for traditional flavor)
- [ ] `¾ cup + 2 tbsp` (200 ml) **cold water** (resolved from [ESTIMATED] — 60% hydration)
- [ ] `1` **large egg**, lightly beaten (optional, for richer dough)

### For Assembly & Cooking
- [ ] `1` **large egg**, beaten with 1 tbsp water (egg wash)
- [ ] **All-purpose flour** — for dusting work surface
- [ ] **Oil for frying** — vegetable or canola, 2 inches deep (if frying)
- [ ] **Coarse sea salt** — for sprinkling after frying/baking

## Instructions

### Component 1: Make the Chimichurri (10 min + 30 min rest)

1. **Combine herbs and aromatics.** In a food processor, pulse cilantro, parsley, garlic, red pepper flakes, salt, and oregano (if using) 8–10× until finely chopped but not puréed — texture should be visible flecks, not paste. *Sensory: bright green, herbaceous aroma hits immediately.*
2. **Emulsify.** With processor running, drizzle in red wine vinegar, then extra-virgin olive oil in a thin stream. Process 10 seconds until glossy and loosely cohesive. *Sensory: oil suspends in vinegar; mixture clings to spoon.*
3. **Rest.** Transfer to a jar; let sit at room temperature 30 minutes (or refrigerate up to 3 days). Flavors meld; heat mellows; oil may separate — stir before using.

### Component 2: Make the Chicken Filling (15 min)

4. **Sauté aromatics.** Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a wide skillet over medium. Add onion and bell pepper; cook 5–6 minutes until softened and onion is translucent, stirring occasionally. *Sensory: sweet onion perfume, no browning.*
5. **Bloom spices.** Add garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, cinnamon; cook 30 seconds until fragrant. *Sensory: warm, earthy, smoky — spices "open up" in hot oil.*
6. **Combine filling.** Add shredded chicken; toss 1 minute to coat in spices. Remove from heat. Stir in ½ cup **drained** chimichurri (press gently against sieve to remove excess oil — see Note), olives, raisins, hard-boiled egg (if using). Season with salt and pepper. *Sensory: filling is moist but not wet; holds shape when squeezed.* Cool completely before assembling (15 min at room temp or 30 min chilled).

### Component 3: Make the Empanada Dough (15 min + 30 min rest)

7. **Mix dry.** In a stand mixer bowl (or large bowl), whisk flour, salt, and baking powder.
8. **Cut in fat.** Add cold cubed butter. Using paddle attachment on low (or fingertips/pastry cutter), mix until mixture resembles coarse sand with pea-sized butter pieces — **do not overmix**. *Sensory: flour coated in fat; butter pieces visible — these create flaky layers.*
9. **Hydrate.** Add cold water (and egg if using) all at once. Mix on low 30 seconds until shaggy dough forms; switch to hook, knead 2 minutes until smooth but not elastic. *Sensory: tacky but not sticky; pulls clean from bowl. Over-kneading develops gluten = tough empanadas.*
10. **Rest.** Form into disk, wrap in plastic, refrigerate 30 minutes (up to 24 hrs). *Critical: rest relaxes gluten for easy rolling; cold fat re-solidifies for flake.*

### Assembly & Cook (25 min)

11. **Roll & cut.** On lightly floured surface, roll dough to ⅛-inch (3 mm) thickness. Cut 4-inch (10 cm) rounds using cutter or inverted bowl. Re-roll scraps once (more = tough). Cover rounds with damp towel.
12. **Fill.** Place 1 heaping tbsp (≈35 g) filling in center of each round. Brush edges lightly with water. Fold into half-moon; press edges to seal. **Crimp (*repulgue*):** starting at one end, fold edge over itself, pressing to create a rope-like seal. *Sensory: sealed edge feels firm, no air pockets.*
13. **CHOOSE METHOD — BAKE:** Preheat oven 400°F (200°C convection). Place empanadas on parchment-lined sheet pan 1 inch apart. Brush with egg wash. Bake 20–22 minutes, rotating halfway, until deep golden and crisp. **FRY:** Heat oil to 350°F (175°C) in heavy pot (thermometer essential). Fry 3–4 empanadas at a time 2–3 minutes per side until golden and puffed. Drain on wire rack; sprinkle immediately with coarse salt.
14. **Rest & serve.** Let baked empanadas rest 5 minutes; fried, 2 minutes. Serve with reserved chimichurri for dipping.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Drain the chimichurri for filling!** The ½ cup chimichurri added to filling must be pressed through a fine sieve to remove excess oil. Undrained chimichurri leaks during bake/fry, causing soggy bottoms and burst seals. Reserve the flavorful oil for drizzling over finished empanadas or the dipping chimichurri.
- **Repulgue (crimp) is structural, not decorative.** The traditional rope crimp creates a mechanical lock that holds under steam pressure. If you can't repulgue, press firmly with a fork *twice* (crosshatch) to seal. Unsealed empanadas burst.
- **Dough temperature matters.** Cold dough (from 30+ min fridge) rolls thinner without springback. Warm dough shrinks, tears, and yields dense pastry. If kitchen is hot, chill rolled rounds 10 min before filling.
- **Filling moisture test.** Squeeze a handful of filling — it should hold shape and leave barely a damp palm. If it drips, add 1 tbsp breadcrumbs or mashed potato to bind. Chimichurri acidity breaks down chicken fibers over time → make filling day-of, or keep chimichurri separate until assembly.

### Variations
- **Empanadas de Carne (Beef):** Substitute 300 g ground beef (browned, fat drained) for chicken; add ¼ cup beef broth while cooking; omit raisins/egg; double cumin.
- **Spinach-Ricotta (Vegetarian):** Omit chicken. Sauté 300 g spinach with garlic; squeeze dry. Mix with 250 g ricotta, 50 g Parmesan, nutmeg, chimichurri (¼ cup). No olives/raisins.
- **Spicy Chorizo-Chicken:** Add 100 g Spanish chorizo, diced and rendered before onions. Reduce salt (chorizo is salty).
- **Baked "Tarta" Style:** Press half dough into 9-inch tart pan; spread filling; top with second rolled sheet; crimp edges; egg wash; bake 35 min at 375°F. Cut into wedges.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Chimichurri:** 5 days refrigerated (oil solidifies — warm jar in water bath to liquefy). 3 months frozen (ice cube trays).
- **Filling:** 2 days refrigerated in airtight. Do not freeze (chimichurri texture degrades).
- **Dough:** 24 hours refrigerated (best); 1 month frozen (thaw overnight in fridge, then rest 1 hr at room temp before rolling).
- **Assembled, unbaked:** 2 hours at room temp (covered with damp towel); 24 hours refrigerated; 1 month frozen on sheet pan → transfer to bag. **Bake/fry from frozen** — add 3–4 min bake or 1 min fry.
- **Cooked empanadas:** Best eaten within 30 min. Reheat baked 350°F 10 min; fried do not reheat well (sog). Freeze baked 1 month; reheat 400°F 15 min from frozen.

### Scaling
- **Golden ratio for dough:** 500 g flour : 200 ml water (40%) + 100 g fat (20% flour weight) + 10 g salt (2%). Scale linearly.
- **Filling per empanada:** 35 g (1 heaping tbsp). 12 empanadas = 420 g filling.
- **Chimichurri ratio:** 1:1:1 herb:herb:vinegar by weight (100g each) : 2× oil (200g). Herbs = 50/50 cilantro/parsley by weight.
- **Large batch (48 empanadas):** Double dough (mix in 2 batches). Quadruple filling. Chimichurri 1× (makes 1 cup → enough for 24 empanadas filling + dip). Fry in 6-batch increments; hold fried at 200°F oven.
