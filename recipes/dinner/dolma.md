# Dolma

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Reconstructed from notebook ingredients: grape leaves, rice, ground lamb, onion, pine nuts, currants, dill, mint, lemon, olive oil, tomato paste.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 4 portions*

- [ ] `1 jar (about 60 leaves)` — grape leaves, rinsed and blanched if necessary
- [ ] `1 cup` — short-grain or medium-grain rice, rinsed
- [ ] `1/2 pound (225g)` — ground lamb
- [ ] `1` — medium onion, finely chopped
- [ ] `1/4 cup` — pine nuts, toasted
- [ ] `1/4 cup` — currants
- [ ] `2 tablespoons` — fresh dill, chopped
- [ ] `2 tablespoons` — fresh mint, chopped
- [ ] `1` — lemon, zest and juice
- [ ] `1/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons` — olive oil
- [ ] `2 tablespoons` — tomato paste
- [ ] `1 1/2 cups` — water (for cooking liquid)
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — salt (adjust to taste)
- [ ] `1/2 teaspoon` — black pepper

---

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the Filling.** In a skillet, heat 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and cook until translucent and softened (~3–4 min). Stir in pine nuts and toast until golden and fragrant (~2 min). Remove from heat and let cool slightly.

2. **Combine Ingredients.** In a large bowl, mix rice, ground lamb, the cooked onion–pine nut mixture, currants, dill, mint, lemon zest, lemon juice, tomato paste, salt, and pepper until evenly combined. The mixture should feel moist and smell herbaceous and bright.

3. **Prepare the Leaves.** If using preserved grape leaves, rinse thoroughly under cold water to remove excess brine. Blanch in boiling water (~2 min) to soften, then drain and pat dry. Trim any tough stems.

4. **Roll the Dolma.** Place a leaf flat, vein side up, with the stem end facing you. Place about 1 tablespoon of filling near the stem end. Fold the sides over the filling, then roll tightly away from you into a cigar shape — snug but not so tight that the rice will burst the leaf as it expands. Repeat with remaining leaves and filling.

5. **Arrange for Cooking.** Line the bottom of a heavy pot with a few torn or imperfect leaves (to prevent sticking). Pack the rolled dolma seam-side down in tight, snug layers. Drizzle the remaining 1/4 cup olive oil over the top.

6. **Add Cooking Liquid.** Mix the water with any remaining tomato paste and pour over the dolma until just covered. Place a heatproof plate on top to weigh the rolls down and keep them submerged.

7. **Simmer.** Bring to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat, then reduce to low. Cook, covered (~35–40 min), until the rice is tender and most of the liquid is absorbed. Avoid boiling vigorously — a gentle bubble keeps the rolls intact.

8. **Rest and Serve.** Remove from heat and let rest 10 minutes before serving. The dolma will firm up slightly as they cool. Serve warm or at room temperature with lemon wedges on the side.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- The notebook specifies grape leaves and lamb; using beef alters the traditional flavor profile. Lamb provides the characteristic richness that defines classic dolma.
- Toasting pine nuts adds a subtle crunch and nutty depth that complements the sweet currants — do not skip this step.

### Variations
- **Vegetarian Dolma:** Omit lamb, increase rice to 1 1/2 cups, add 1/2 cup chopped toasted walnuts and 1 tablespoon pomegranate molasses to the filling for a sweet-tart balance.

### Make-Ahead
- Dolma tastes better after resting; refrigerate overnight and reheat gently or serve cold the next day.
- Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.

### Storage
- Freeze cooked dolma in a single layer on a tray, then transfer to a freezer bag for up to 2 months. Thaw in the refrigerator before reheating gently on the stovetop or in the microwave.

### Scaling
- The filling-to-leaf ratio is key; maintain approximately 1 tbsp filling per leaf.
- For larger batches, use a wider pot to avoid stacking too high; adjust cooking time slightly if needed.
