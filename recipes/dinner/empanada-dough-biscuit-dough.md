# Empanada Dough / Biscuit Dough

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Shared dough note from notebook page 19. A versatile, flaky dough that works for both empanadas and biscuit-style pastries — savory or sweet.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 1 batch*

- [ ] `3 cups` — all-purpose flour
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — salt
- [ ] `1 cup (2 sticks)` — unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small cubes
- [ ] `1/2 to 3/4 cup` — ice-cold water

---

## Instructions

1. **Combine Dry Ingredients.** In a large mixing bowl, whisk together 3 cups flour and 1 teaspoon salt until evenly distributed.

2. **Cut in the Butter.** Add the chilled butter cubes to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter, two forks, or your fingertips, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse meal with pea-sized butter pieces visible throughout (~3–4 min). The butter should remain cold and firm — if it starts to soften, chill the bowl for 10 minutes.

3. **Add Cold Water.** Drizzle in ice-cold water, 1 tablespoon at a time, while tossing the mixture with a fork. Start with 1/2 cup and add more only as needed. The dough should just come together when pressed between your fingers — it will look shaggy but should hold its shape (~1–2 min).

4. **Form and Rest.** Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and gather into a rough disk. Do not knead — overworking develops gluten and makes the dough tough. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling relaxes the gluten and re-solidifies the butter, ensuring a flaky result.

5. **Roll and Use.** After resting, roll the dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness — about 1/8 inch for empanadas, 1/2 inch for biscuits. Cut into rounds or shapes as needed. The dough should feel smooth and pliable, with visible flecks of butter throughout.

6. **Bake or Fry.** For empanadas: fill, seal, and bake at 375°F (190°C) until golden brown (~20–25 min) or fry at 350°F (175°C) until crisp (~3–4 min). For biscuits: cut into rounds and bake at 425°F (220°C) until tall and golden (~12–15 min).

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- Keeping the butter cold is essential for flakiness — if the dough feels warm at any point, refrigerate for 10 minutes before continuing.
- This dough is intentionally flexible; the notebook uses one template for multiple applications. For a richer dough, substitute up to 1/4 cup of the water with cold milk or buttermilk.

### Variations
- **Herb Dough:** Add 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh herbs (such as rosemary, thyme, or parsley) to the flour mixture for savory applications.

### Make-Ahead
- The dough can be made up to 2 days ahead and refrigerated, tightly wrapped.
- For longer storage, freeze the wrapped dough disk for up to 3 months; thaw in the refrigerator overnight before using.

### Storage
- Refrigerate wrapped dough for up to 2 days.
- Freeze for up to 3 months; thaw in refrigerator.

### Scaling
- The dough scales linearly; maintain the ratio of 3 parts flour to 1 part butter.
- Add liquid slowly — the dough should not get wet. A little extra flour on the work surface is better than a sticky dough.
