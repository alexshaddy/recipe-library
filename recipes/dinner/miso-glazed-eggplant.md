# Miso-Glazed Eggplant

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Title-only addendum; recipe created from nasu dengaku technique.

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 2 halves (1 small eggplant)*

### For the Miso Glaze
- 2 tablespoons white miso (shiro miso) or awase (mixed) miso
- 1 tablespoon mirin
- 1 tablespoon sake
- 1 teaspoon sugar (or to taste)
- 1 teaspoon water (to adjust consistency if needed)

### For the Eggplant
- 1 small Japanese eggplant or ½ large globe eggplant (about 8 oz/225g), halved lengthwise
- 1 teaspoon neutral oil (such as grapeseed or canola)
- Pinch of salt

### For Garnish (optional)
- 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds
- 2 thin slices scallion (green part only), finely sliced

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the Eggplant** – Preheat broiler (or grill) to high. Place eggplant halves cut-side up on a baking sheet. Score the flesh in a diamond pattern, being careful not to cut through the skin. Brush the cut side lightly with oil and sprinkle with a pinch of salt.
2. **Make the Miso Glaze** – In a small bowl, whisk together miso, mirin, sake, and sugar until smooth. If the paste is too thick, whisk in water a teaspoon at a time until it reaches a spreadable consistency.
3. **Initial Broil** – Place the eggplant under the broiler, about 4 inches from the heat source. Cook until the surface begins to blister and brown in spots, 3–4 minutes. Watch closely to avoid burning.
4. **Apply Glaze** – Remove the eggplant from the broiler. Using a brush, generously coat the scored flesh with the miso glaze, ensuring it seeps into the cuts.
5. **Glaze and Broil Again** – Return the eggplant to the broiler. Cook until the glaze bubbles and caramelizes, turning a deep glossy brown, 3–5 minutes. The edges may darken; this adds flavor.
6. **Rest** – Remove from the broiler and let rest for 2–3 minutes. The glaze will set slightly.
7. **Garnish and Serve** – Sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds and scallion if desired. Serve warm or at room temperature.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- Scoring the eggplant allows the glaze to penetrate and ensures even cooking.
- Watch the glaze closely during the final broil; it can go from caramelized to burnt quickly due to the sugar content.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- The miso glaze can be made ahead and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.
- Grilled eggplant is best served immediately but can be kept at room temperature for up to 2 hours; reheat gently if needed.
- Leftovers store in the refrigerator for up to 2 days; reheat in a toaster oven or microwave (glaze may soften).

### Scaling
- Double or triple the glaze recipe as needed; maintain the 3:2:1 miso:mirin:sake ratio and adjust sugar to taste.
- For larger eggplants, increase halving quantity accordingly; adjust broiling time slightly based on thickness.

### Variations
- **Spicy Miso Glaze:** Add ½ teaspoon chili garlic sauce or sriracha to the glaze for a kick.
- **Miso-Tahini Blend:** Replace half the miso with tahini for a nutty, creamy variation (reduce salt if needed).
