# Savory Meat Loaf

> [!NOTE] Origin
> From a Carnation evaporated milk pamphlet headed "5 Ground Beef Ideas." This version adds horseradish for a sharper, more savory finish.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 6 slices*

### Beef Mixture

- [ ] `2/3 cup` — undiluted evaporated milk
- [ ] `1 1/2 pounds` — ground chuck
- [ ] `1/2 cup` — fine cracker meal
- [ ] `1` — egg
- [ ] `1/2 cup` — onion, chopped
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — prepared mustard
- [ ] `1 1/2 teaspoons` — salt
- [ ] `1/8 teaspoon` — black pepper

### Savory Additions

- [ ] `1/3 cup` — evaporated milk, undiluted
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — prepared horseradish

---

## Instructions

1. **Season the Meat Mixture.** Combine the beef mixture ingredients with the additional evaporated milk and horseradish, mixing lightly with your hands or a fork until just combined (~1–2 min). Overmixing will make the loaf dense and tough.

2. **Pack the Pan.** Transfer the mixture to a 9×5×3-inch loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula. The surface should be even so it browns uniformly.

3. **Bake Until Done.** Bake at 350°F until the loaf is browned on top, pulls away slightly from the sides, and an instant-read thermometer registers 160°F in the center (~1 hr). The aroma will fill the kitchen with savory, beefy notes.

4. **Rest Briefly.** Let the loaf stand for 5–10 minutes before slicing so the juices redistribute and the slices hold together cleanly. Slice and serve hot.

---

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- The horseradish reads as a small amount, but it gives the loaf a cleaner savory edge than extra salt would.
- Mixing the meat lightly is key — overworking develops tough proteins. Stop as soon as the ingredients are evenly distributed.

### Variations
- **Milder loaf:** Reduce the horseradish slightly if you want less bite.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- The loaf can be mixed ahead and refrigerated before baking.
- Leftovers keep well for sandwiches the next day.

### Scaling
- The recipe behaves like a standard meat loaf once the beef mixture is mixed lightly and not overworked.
