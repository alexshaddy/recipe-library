# Yakiniku Salt Slab

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Notebook dinner note from the 5.13 Kat dinner page. A5 Wagyu grilled tableside on a Himalayan salt slab with bordelaise and negi.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 4 portions*

### For the A5 Wagyu

- [ ] `1 pound` — A5 Wagyu beef, thinly sliced (for yakiniku)

### For the Himalayan Salt Slab

- [ ] `1` — Himalayan salt slab (approximately 10×6×1 inch) — *equipment, not consumed*

### For the Bordelaise Sauce

- [ ] `3/4 cup` — dry red wine (such as Bordeaux)
- [ ] `1/2 cup` — beef stock
- [ ] `1` — shallot, finely minced
- [ ] `1 sprig` — fresh thyme (or ¼ tsp dried thyme)
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — unsalted butter
- [ ] `to taste` — salt and freshly ground black pepper

### For the Nori Bordelaise Variant *(optional)*

- [ ] `1 sheet` — nori, finely shredded

### For the Negi Bed

- [ ] `2` — negi (Welsh onion) or scallions, white and light green parts only, thinly sliced lengthwise
- [ ] `a few drops` — neutral oil
- [ ] `pinch` — salt

### For Serving

- [ ] `to taste` — flaky sea salt (for finishing)
- [ ] `to taste` — lemon wedges (optional)

---

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the Bordelaise.** In a small saucepan, combine red wine, beef stock, minced shallot, and thyme. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and reduce until slightly thickened and volume is halved — the sauce should coat the back of a spoon (~8–10 min). Remove thyme sprig, whisk in butter until glossy and emulsified, then season with salt and pepper. Keep warm. *(Optional: stir in shredded nori for nori bordelaise variant.)*

2. **Make the Negi Bed.** Slice negi lengthwise into thin ribbons. Toss with a few drops of neutral oil and a pinch of salt. Set aside at room temperature — the ribbons will soften slightly and release a mild onion fragrance.

3. **Heat the Salt Slab.** Place the Himalayan salt slab on a grill over medium-high heat. Allow it to heat gradually for 15–20 minutes until the surface is hot — a few drops of water should sizzle and evaporate instantly on contact. Do not overheat or the slab may crack; a gentle, even preheat is essential.

4. **Grill the Wagyu.** Arrange sliced A5 Wagyu in a single layer on the hot salt slab. Grill 1–2 minutes per side, depending on thickness, until the edges are browned and crisp but the center is still pink and tender. The meat will sizzle loudly and release a rich, beefy aroma as it cooks.

5. **Rest the Meat.** Transfer grilled Wagyu to a warm plate and let rest for 2 minutes — this allows the juices to settle and the salt seasoning from the slab to distribute evenly.

6. **Assemble the Negi Bed.** Spread the negi ribbons across a serving platter or individual plates in an even layer.

7. **Serve.** Place rested Wagyu atop the negi bed. Drizzle generously with warm bordelaise (or nori bordelaise). Finish with a light sprinkle of flaky sea salt and serve with lemon wedges on the side. Eat immediately while the salt slab retains residual heat — the negi will wilt gently under the warm meat.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- Gradual heating of the salt slab is critical — rapid temperature changes can cause thermal shock and crack the slab. Always start on medium heat and increase gradually.
- A5 Wagyu is prized for its intense marbling; the fat renders quickly on the hot salt, creating a self-basting effect. If substituting with a leaner cut, brush lightly with oil before grilling to prevent sticking.

### Variations
- **Nori Bordelaise:** Stir 1 finely shredded nori sheet into the finished bordelaise for a subtle oceanic umami that complements the beef.
- **Miso-Bordelaise:** Whisk 1 tablespoon white miso into the bordelaise at the end (off heat) for a deeper, savory-sweet dimension.

### Make-Ahead
- The bordelaise can be made up to 2 days ahead and refrigerated. Reheat gently over low heat, whisking in a splash of water or stock if it has thickened too much.
- Negi ribbons can be sliced a few hours ahead and held in ice water to stay crisp; drain and pat dry before tossing with oil and salt.

### Storage
- Grilled Wagyu is best eaten immediately; leftovers can be refrigerated for up to 1 day and gently reheated, though the texture will change.
- Bordelaise keeps refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 3 days, or frozen for up to 1 month.

### Scaling
- The recipe scales linearly for meat and sauces. For larger groups, use a larger salt slab or work in batches — overcrowding the slab will steam the meat instead of searing it.
- Double the bordelaise if you want extra for drizzling over rice or vegetables on the side.
