# Berries and Cream Highball

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Working title for a handwritten bar note from the inbox scan set.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 1 cocktail*

- [ ] `2 ounces` — **strawberry-infused vodka** (or strawberry liqueur such as Crème de Fraise; resolved from "strawberry spirit or strawberry base [UNCLEAR]")
- [ ] `1/2 ounce` — fresh lemon juice
- [ ] `1/2 ounce` — fresh lime juice
- [ ] `3/4 ounce` — **strawberry purée** (fresh or frozen strawberries blended and strained; resolved from "berry component [UNCLEAR]")
- [ ] `1/2 ounce` — **heavy cream** (36–40% fat; resolved from "cream component [UNCLEAR]")
- [ ] `1` — egg white (or 15 ml aquafaba for vegan)
- [ ] `3 drops` — **Angostura bitters** (classic aromatic; resolved from "aromatic bitters or similar [UNCLEAR]")
- [ ] `~2–3 ounces` — soda water, to top
- [ ] Ice — for shaking (cubes) and serving (one large cube or pebble ice)

---

## Instructions

1. **Dry-shake the foam.** Add strawberry-infused vodka, lemon juice, lime juice, strawberry purée, heavy cream, egg white, and Angostura bitters to a shaker *without ice*. Shake vigorously for 15–20 seconds to emulsify the egg white and cream.
2. **Wet-shake to chill.** Add ice cubes and shake hard for 10–12 seconds until the tin is frosted.
3. **Build in glass.** Fine-strain into a Collins glass filled with fresh pebble ice or one large cube.
4. **Top and integrate.** Gently top with soda water (~2–3 oz). Give one brief stir with a bar spoon to integrate the foam cap.
5. **Garnish.** Express a lemon twist over the drink and rest it on the foam; optionally add a small fresh strawberry on the rim.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Strawberry-infused vodka resolution:** The original "[UNCLEAR] strawberry spirit or strawberry base" most plausibly indicates a house-infused strawberry vodka (common at Mockingbird Bar for highballs) rather than a commercial strawberry liqueur, which would be overly sweet. If using a liqueur (e.g., Crème de Fraise), reduce to 1 oz and cut the purée to ½ oz to balance sugar.
- **Cream component resolution:** "Cream component [UNCLEAR]" in a shaken highball with egg white almost certainly means heavy cream (36–40%) for body and stable foam; half-and-half would produce a loose foam, and milk would curdle with the citrus.
- **Berry component resolution:** "Berry component [UNCLEAR]" resolved to strawberry purée to match the drink name and the strawberry spirit base. A mixed-berry purée would work but shifts the profile.
- **Bitters resolution:** Three drops of Angostura is the standard aromatic dash for a cream-based shaken drink; it adds spice backbone without dominating.
- **Method rationale:** The dry-shake → wet-shake sequence is essential for egg-white drinks. Building over pebble ice in a Collins glass (highball method) provides the correct dilution curve (~25–30% total dilution) and showcases the foam cap.

### Variations
- **Berry & Cream Highball (mixed berry):** Use mixed-berry purée and a neutral vodka; garnish with a blackberry.
- **Raspberry Cream Collins:** Substitute raspberry purée and raspberry liqueur (Chambord or Crème de Framboise) for the strawberry elements; use lemon only (no lime).
- **Nondairy / Vegan:** Replace heavy cream with ½ oz coconut cream + ¼ oz oat milk; swap egg white for 15 ml aquafaba. Dry-shake longer (25 sec) for foam stability.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Batch base (no egg white, no soda):** Combine infused vodka, citrus, purée, cream, and bitters. Refrigerate up to 24 hours in a sealed container. Do not batch with egg white — it weeps and loses foam structure.
- **To serve:** Measure 4 oz batched base per drink, add egg white, dry-shake, wet-shake, fine-strain over fresh ice, top with soda.

### Scaling
- **Linear scaling:** Multiply all base ingredients (except egg white and soda) by the number of servings.
- **Egg white:** One egg white serves 2–3 drinks; for larger batches, use 1 egg white per 3 servings and dry-shake the full batch, then portion and wet-shake individually.
- **Soda:** Always top per glass to preserve carbonation.
