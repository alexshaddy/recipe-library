# Black Tea Orange Punch

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Handwritten guest-check recipe from the Mockingbird Bar scan set (assets/loose_notes_scans/brw849e563cc961_000403.jpg). A citrus-tea punch served in a mug — the "black tea syrup" is the signature component. Can be served hot (traditional punch) or iced.

---

## Ingredients

**Black Tea Syrup (make ahead, ~1 cup):**
- `1 cup` — water
- `1 cup` — rich demerara syrup (2:1 demerara sugar:water)
- `3 tbsp` — loose-leaf black tea (Assam or Ceylon; robust, malty)
- `1 strip` — orange peel (3" x 1", no pith)
- `1 strip` — lemon peel (3" x 1", no pith)
- `1` — cinnamon stick (3")
- `3` — whole allspice berries
- `1` — star anise (optional)

**Per Cocktail (served hot in mug):**
- [ ] `1 1/2 ounces` — **black tea syrup** (resolved from "syrup component, likely black tea or prune-style syrup [UNCLEAR]" — the scan's "prune-style" suggests dark, spiced tea syrup; black tea + demerara + citrus peel + baking spices matches the flavor profile)
- [ ] `1/2 ounce` — **fresh lemon juice** (from ½ lemon; the scan's "1 lemon" = juice of half for single serve)
- [ ] `1/2 ounce` — **fresh orange juice** (from ½ orange; the scan's "1 orange" = juice of half)
- [ ] `2 dashes` — **orange bitters** (Angostura orange or Regans'; resolved from "orange bitters — to taste")
- [ ] `3 ounces` — **hot water** (just off boil, ~200°F / 93°C; the "weak" component in punch formula)
- [ ] **Garnish:** orange wheel studded with 2 whole cloves, cinnamon stick stirrer
- [ ] **Glassware:** pre-warmed heavy mug or Irish coffee mug or thick ceramic mug

---

## Instructions

### Make Black Tea Syrup (yields ~1 cup, enough for 8–10 drinks):
1. **Steep.** Bring water to a simmer; remove from heat. Add black tea, orange peel, lemon peel, cinnamon, allspice, star anise (if using). Steep 5 minutes.
2. **Strain.** Fine-strain through cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean jar.
3. **Sweeten.** Stir in rich demerara syrup until dissolved. Cool, then refrigerate up to 2 weeks.

### Per Drink (Hot Service):
1. **Pre-warm mug.** Fill mug with hot water; let sit 30 seconds; discard water.
2. **Build in mug.** Add black tea syrup, lemon juice, orange juice, and orange bitters to the warmed mug.
3. **Add hot water.** Pour 3 oz hot water; stir gently with bar spoon to integrate.
4. **Garnish.** Add orange wheel studded with cloves; provide cinnamon stick as stirrer.

### Per Drink (Iced Service):
1. **Build in shaker.** Add black tea syrup, lemon juice, orange juice, orange bitters, and 3 oz cold water to a shaker tin.
2. **Shake briefly.** Add ice; shake 5 seconds to chill (no dilution target beyond chill).
3. **Strain.** Pour into a mug or rocks glass over pebble ice.
4. **Garnish.** Orange wheel, cinnamon stick.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Syrup resolution:** The scan's "[UNCLEAR] syrup component, likely black tea or prune-style syrup" — "prune-style" indicates a dark, molasses-rich, spiced syrup. Black tea steeped in demerara syrup with citrus peel and baking spices (cinnamon, allspice) produces exactly this: dark amber, malty, citrus-oiled, spiced. Prune syrup would be a separate ingredient; the scan likely misread the dark tea syrup as "prune-style."
- **Punch formula:** This follows the classic 2:1:1:1 (strong:sour:sweet:weak) *per serving*, where:
  - **Strong:** The base spirit is absent — this is a *temperance* or *low-ABV* punch relying on tea tannins for structure. If adding spirit, 1 oz aged rum or bourbon fits.
  - **Sour:** ½ oz lemon + ½ oz orange = 1 oz total citrus.
  - **Sweet:** 1.5 oz black tea syrup.
  - **Weak:** 3 oz hot water (or cold water for iced).
  - Ratio ≈ 0:1:1.5:3 — a "weak" punch, correcting with tea tannins for body.
- **Mug service:** "Mug" in the scan indicates hot service default. Pre-warming the mug is essential — cold mug shocks the drink and kills aromatics.
- **Bitters "to taste":** Standardized to 2 dashes per serving for consistency.

### Variations
- **Spirited Black Tea Punch:** Add 1 oz aged Jamaican rum (Appleton 12) or bourbon per serving; reduce hot water to 2 oz.
- **Earl Grey Variant:** Use Earl Grey tea (bergamot) instead of black tea; omit orange peel from syrup.
- **Spiced Prune Syrup:** For a true "prune-style" syrup: simmer ½ cup prunes in 1 cup water + 1 cup demerara syrup + spices 20 min; strain. Richer, fruitier.
- **Batch for Party (Hot, 10 servings):** 15 oz tea syrup, 5 oz lemon, 5 oz orange, 20 dashes bitters, 30 oz hot water. Hold in thermos or slow-cooker on warm.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Black Tea Syrup:** Refrigerate up to 2 weeks in sealed jar. Freezes well (ice cube tray, 1 oz portions).
- **Citrus juice:** Fresh-squeeze day-of. Pre-juiced citrus oxidizes in 4–6 hours.
- **Batch base (cold, no water):** Combine syrup, lemon, orange, bitters. Refrigerate up to 24 hours. Add hot/cold water per serving.

### Scaling
- **Per drink:** Linear scaling — all ingredients multiply 1:1.
- **Batch (hot):** Multiply per-drink quantities by serving count. Hold in insulated vessel. Do not pre-dilute with water until service.
- **Batch (cold):** Multiply, add 20% water by volume (pre-dilution), chill 4+ hours, serve over pebble ice.
