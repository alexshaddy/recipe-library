# Ika Punch

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Handwritten guest-check recipe from the Mockingbird Bar scan set (assets/loose_notes_scans/brw849e563cc961_000403.jpg). "Ika" is legible on the card — likely a house nickname or bartender's shorthand for this prune-black-tea mug punch. Distinct from the Citrus Mug Punch and Black Tea Orange Punch by its prune-forward syrup.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 1 cocktail (hot) or 1 cocktail (iced)*

**Prune-Black-Tea Syrup (make ahead — yields ~1 cup / 8–10 servings):**
- [ ] `1 cup` — water
- [ ] `2 tbsp` — loose black tea (Assam or Ceylon) or 3 tea bags
- [ ] `½ cup` — pitted prunes (chopped)
- [ ] `1 strip` — orange peel (1" x 3", no pith)
- [ ] `1 strip` — lemon peel (1" x 3", no pith)
- [ ] `1 stick` — cinnamon (3")
- [ ] `4` — whole allspice berries
- [ ] `1` — star anise
- [ ] `1 cup` — rich demerara syrup (2:1 demerara sugar : water)

**Per Drink (Hot):**
- [ ] `1.5 oz` — **prune-black-tea syrup** (resolved from "syrup — likely prune-black-tea syrup or similar [UNCLEAR]" — the scan's "prune-black-tea" is specifically legible; see cook's notes)
- [ ] `0.5 oz` — fresh lemon juice
- [ ] `0.5 oz` — fresh orange juice
- [ ] `3 oz` — hot water
- [ ] `2 dashes` — orange bitters (Angostura orange or Fee Brothers)
- [ ] **Garnish:** orange wheel studded with 2 whole cloves, cinnamon stick

**Per Drink (Iced):**
- [ ] `1.5 oz` — prune-black-tea syrup
- [ ] `0.5 oz` — fresh lemon juice
- [ ] `0.5 oz` — fresh orange juice
- [ ] `2 oz` — cold water
- [ ] `2 dashes` — orange bitters
- [ ] Ice — pebble or large cube
- [ ] **Garnish:** orange wheel, mint sprig (slapped)

---

## Instructions

### Make Prune-Black-Tea Syrup (yields ~1 cup, enough for 8–10 drinks):
1. **Steep prunes & tea.** Bring water to a simmer; add prunes, tea, orange peel, lemon peel, cinnamon, allspice, star anise. Simmer 10 minutes (prunes soften and release flavor).
2. **Strain.** Fine-strain through cheesecloth or coffee filter, pressing prunes to extract liquid.
3. **Sweeten.** Stir in demerara syrup until dissolved. Cool, then refrigerate up to 2 weeks.

### Hot Service (Standard):
1. **Pre-warm mug.** Fill mug with hot water; let sit 30 seconds; discard water.
2. **Build.** Add prune-black-tea syrup, lemon juice, orange juice, orange bitters to the warm mug.
3. **Dilute.** Pour 3 oz hot water; stir gently with bar spoon to integrate.
4. **Garnish.** Orange wheel studded with 2 whole cloves; cinnamon stick as stirrer.

### Iced Service:
1. **Build in shaker.** Add prune-black-tea syrup, lemon juice, orange juice, orange bitters, cold water.
2. **Chill.** Add ice; shake 5 seconds until tin frosts.
3. **Strain.** Pour into mug or rocks glass over fresh pebble ice.
4. **Garnish.** Orange wheel; mint sprig slapped between palms.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Syrup resolution:** The scan's "syrup — likely prune-black-tea syrup or similar [UNCLEAR]" is the most specific syrup note in the whole set. "Prune-black-tea" is legible as a compound descriptor — not "prune or black tea" but "prune-black-tea" as a single combined syrup. Prunes + black tea + baking spices in demerara syrup creates a deeply fruity, malty, spiced dark syrup with raisin/fig notes from prunes and tannin structure from tea. This is the signature "Ika" syrup.
- **Title "Ika":** Unknown origin — possibly a bartender's nickname, a regular's name, or shorthand for "Ikaika" (Hawaiian for "strong"). The drink is strong on flavor, not ABV.
- **Punch formula (hot, per drink):** Syrup (sweet) 1.5 oz : Lemon 0.5 oz + Orange 0.5 oz (sour 1 oz) : Water (weak) 3 oz. No spirit — temperance punch. Prune/tea tannins provide structure.
- **Mug + straw:** The scan notes "mug or heavy glass" and "straw" — straw for hot service was a Mockingbird Bar signature (sip without burning lip; straw reaches under the foam/spice layer). Pre-warming mug is essential.

### Variations
- **Spirited Ika Punch:** Add 1 oz dark rum (Hamilton 86) or bourbon per serving; reduce hot water to 2 oz.
- **Port-Washed Ika:** Stir ½ oz ruby port into hot finished drink; adds berry depth.
- **Non-Prune Variant:** Use date syrup (date paste + water + spices) instead of prunes — similar flavor profile, different fruit.
- **Batch Hot (10 servings):** 15 oz prune-tea syrup, 5 oz lemon, 5 oz orange, 20 dashes orange bitters, 30 oz hot water. Hold in thermos or slow-cooker on warm.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Prune-Black-Tea Syrup:** Refrigerate up to 2 weeks; freezes well (ice cube tray, 1.5 oz cubes). Flavor deepens over 24 hours.
- **Citrus juice:** Fresh-squeeze day-of. Pre-juiced oxidizes in 4–6 hours.
- **Batch base (no water):** Combine syrup, lemon, orange, bitters. Refrigerate up to 24 hours. Add water per serving.

### Scaling
- **Per drink:** Linear — all ingredients scale 1:1.
- **Hot batch:** Multiply syrup, citrus, bitters. Hold water separate, add at service.
- **Cold batch:** Multiply all including water by count. Add 20% extra water for pre-dilution. Chill 4+ hours. Serve over pebble ice.
