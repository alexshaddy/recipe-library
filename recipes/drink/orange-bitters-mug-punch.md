# Orange Bitters Mug Punch

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Handwritten guest-check recipe from the Mockingbird Bar scan set (assets/loose_notes_scans/brw849e563cc961_000403.jpg). Title is a working title — the card prominently features orange bitters as the defining aromatic. Same prune-black-tea syrup base as Ika Punch, but with doubled orange bitters and orange-forward citrus ratio.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 1 cocktail (hot) or 1 cocktail (iced)*

**Prune-Black-Tea Syrup (make ahead — yields ~1 cup / 8–10 servings):**
- [ ] `1 cup` — water
- [ ] `2 tbsp` — loose black tea (Assam or Ceylon) or 3 tea bags
- [ ] `½ cup` — pitted prunes (chopped)
- [ ] `1 strip` — orange peel (1" x 3", no pith)
- [ ] `1 strip` — lemon peel (1" x 3", no pith)
- [ ] `1 stick` — cinnamon (3")
- [ ] `4` — whole allspice berries
- [ ] `1` — star anise
- [ ] `1 cup` — rich demerara syrup (2:1 demerara sugar : water)

**Per Drink (Hot):**
- [ ] `1.5 oz` — **prune-black-tea syrup** (resolved from "syrup — likely prune-black-tea syrup or similar [UNCLEAR]" — same house syrup as Ika Punch; see cook's notes)
- [ ] `0.75 oz` — fresh orange juice (increased vs lemon; "orange bitters" title = orange-forward)
- [ ] `0.25 oz` — fresh lemon juice
- [ ] `3 oz` — hot water
- [ ] `4 dashes` — **orange bitters** (Angostura orange or Fee Brothers; doubled from standard 2 dashes per the title's emphasis)
- [ ] **Garnish:** orange peel coin (expressed, rimmed), candied orange peel thread

**Per Drink (Iced):**
- [ ] `1.5 oz` — prune-black-tea syrup
- [ ] `0.75 oz` — fresh orange juice
- [ ] `0.25 oz` — fresh lemon juice
- [ ] `2 oz` — cold water
- [ ] `4 dashes` — orange bitters
- [ ] Ice — pebble or large cube
- [ ] **Garnish:** orange wheel, orange peel twist

---

## Instructions

### Make Prune-Black-Tea Syrup (yields ~1 cup, enough for 8–10 drinks):
1. **Steep prunes & tea.** Bring water to a simmer; add prunes, tea, orange peel, lemon peel, cinnamon, allspice, star anise. Simmer 10 minutes.
2.2. **Strain.** Fine-strain through cheesecloth or coffee filter, pressing prunes to extract liquid.
3. **Sweeten.** Stir in demerara syrup until dissolved. Cool, then refrigerate up to 2 weeks.

### Hot Service (Standard):
1. **Pre-warm mug.** Fill mug with hot water; let sit 30 seconds; discard water.
2. **Build.** Add prune-black-tea syrup, orange juice, lemon juice, orange bitters to the warm mug.
3. **Dilute.** Pour 3 oz hot water; stir gently with bar spoon to integrate.
4. **Garnish.** Express orange peel coin over drink, rim the glass, drop in; add candied orange peel thread.

### Iced Service:
1. **Build in shaker.** Add prune-black-tea syrup, orange juice, lemon juice, orange bitters, cold water.
2. **Chill.** Add ice; shake 5 seconds until tin frosts.e
3. **Strain.** Pour into mug or rocks glass over fresh pebble ice.
4. **Garnish.** Orange wheel; orange peel twist expressed and dropped in.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Syrup resolution:** The scan's "[UNCLEAR] syrup — likely prune-black-tea syrup or similar" resolves to the same house prune-black-tea syrup as Ika Punch. Mockingbird Bar used one batch of this syrup for multiple mug punch variants. The "orange bitters" title distinguishes this version by its aromatics, not its sweetener.
- **Orange-forward citrus:** Unlike Citrus Mug Punch (lemon-only) and Ika Punch (equal lemon/orange), this recipe uses ¾ oz orange : ¼ oz lemon — orange is the star, matching the 4 dashes of orange bitters.
- **Double orange bitters:** Standard mug punch gets 2 dashes orange bitters; this gets 4. The bitters are a featured ingredient, not just seasoning. Fee Brothers West Indian Orange Bitters (more citrus-oil forward) preferred over Angostura Orange here.
- **No straw in hot version:** The scan notes "straw" but for a hot, aromatic drink with expressed peel oils on the surface, a straw bypasses the nose. Serve without straw for hot; with straw for iced (sip through ice).

### Variations
- **Bitter-Orange Amaro Float:** Float ¼ oz amaro (Aperol, Cynar, or Nonino) on top of hot finished drink; creates a bitter-orange layer.
- **Spirited Version:** Add 1 oz bourbon or dark rum; reduce hot water to 2 oz. The bitters amplify the spirit's oak.
- **Grapefruit-Orange Split:** Use ½ oz orange + ½ oz grapefruit juice; add 2 dashes grapefruit bitters + 2 dashes orange bitters.
- **Batch Hot (10 servings):** 15 oz prune-tea syrup, 7.5 oz orange, 2.5 oz lemon, 40 dashes orange bitters, 30 oz hot water. Hold in thermos.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Prune-Black-Tea Syrup:** Refrigerate up to 2 weeks; freezes well (ice cube tray, 1.5 oz cubes).
- **Citrus juice:** Fresh-squeeze day-of. Orange juice oxidizes faster than lemon — squeeze à la minute if possible.
- **Batch base (no water):** Combine syrup, orange, lemon, bitters. Refrigerate up to 12 hours (bitters soften). Add water per serving.

### Scaling
- **Per drink:** Linear — all ingredients scale 1:1.
- **Hot batch:** Multiply syrup, citrus, bitters. Hold water separate, add at service.
- **Cold batch:** Multiply all including water by count. Add 20% extra water for pre-dilution. Chill 4+ hours. Serve over pebble ice.
