# Strawberry Cream Collins

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Handwritten guest-check recipe from the Mockingbird Bar scan set (assets/loose_notes_scans/brw849e563cc961_000402.jpg). The card shows a classic Cream Collins structure: base spirit, lemon, sugar/syrup, cream, egg white, soda top. The berry element identifies this as a strawberry variant.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 1 cocktail*

- [ ] `2 ounces` — **strawberry-infused gin** (house-infused; resolved from "strawberry component or strawberry spirit [UNCLEAR]")
- [ ] `3/4 ounce` — fresh lemon juice
- [ ] `1/2 ounce` — **rich simple syrup (2:1)** (resolved from "sweetener [UNCLEAR]" — a Collins requires sugar syrup)
- [ ] `3/4 ounce` — **strawberry purée** (fresh or frozen strawberries blended and fine-strained; resolved from "strawberry purée or strawberry component [UNCLEAR]")
- [ ] `1/2 ounce` — **heavy cream** (36–40%; resolved from "cream [UNCLEAR]")
- [ ] `1` — egg white (or 15 ml aquafaba for vegan)
- [ ] `3 drops` — **peach bitters** (Peychaud's or Angostura; resolved from "aromatic bitters or similar [UNCLEAR]")
- [ ] `~2–3 ounces` — soda water, to top
- [ ] Ice — for shaking (cubes) and serving (one large cube or pebble ice)
- [ ] **Garnish:** lemon twist expressed over the drink, small fresh strawberry on rim

---

## Instructions

1. **Dry-shake the foam.** Add strawberry-infused gin, lemon juice, rich simple syrup, strawberry purée, heavy cream, egg white, and peach bitters to a shaker *without ice*. Shake vigorously for 15–20 seconds to emulsify the egg white and cream.
2. **Wet-shake to chill.** Add ice cubes and shake hard for 10–12 seconds until the tin is frosted.
3. **Build in glass.** Fine-strain into a Collins glass filled with fresh pebble ice or one large cube.
4. **Top and integrate.** Gently top with soda water (~2–3 oz). Give one brief stir with a bar spoon to integrate the foam cap.
5. **Garnish.** Express a lemon twist over the drink and rest it on the foam; add a small fresh strawberry on the rim.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Base spirit resolution:** The original "[UNCLEAR] strawberry component or strawberry spirit" in a Collins context most likely indicates a house-infused strawberry gin (Mockingbird Bar's house gin was the default for Collins variations). Gin's juniper backbone balances the cream and berry better than vodka. If strawberry liqueur (Crème de Fraise) is used instead, reduce to 1 oz and omit the simple syrup.
- **Sweetener resolution:** A Collins *requires* a sugar component (the "sweet" in sour-sweet-strong-weak). The scan's "[UNCLEAR]" at the sweetener position resolves to rich simple syrup (2:1) at ½ oz — the standard Collins ratio (2:¾:½ spirit:lemon:sugar) adjusted for the added purée sweetness.
- **Berry component resolution:** "Berry component [UNCLEAR]" alongside a named "strawberry spirit" base resolves to strawberry purée (not a second liqueur) to provide fruit texture and color without doubling the sugar.
- **Cream resolution:** Heavy cream (36–40%) is standard for Cream Collins; it stabilizes the egg-white foam and provides the signature mouthfeel. Half-and-half produces a loose foam; milk curdles.
- **Bitters resolution:** Peach bitters (e.g., Peychaud's) complement strawberry better than Angostura; three drops is a standard aromatic dash.
- **Method rationale:** Dry-shake → wet-shake is mandatory for egg-white drinks. Building over pebble ice in a Collins glass (highball method) yields ~25–30% dilution and the classic foam cap.

### Variations
- **Strawberry Vodka Collins:** Use strawberry-infused vodka for a cleaner, fruit-forward profile; keep peach bitters.
- **Raspberry Cream Collins:** Substitute raspberry purée and raspberry-infused gin; use lemon only (no bitters needed).
- **Nondairy / Vegan:** Replace heavy cream with ½ oz coconut cream + ¼ oz oat milk; swap egg white for 15 ml aquafaba. Dry-shake longer (25 sec).

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Batch base (no egg white, no soda, no bitters):** Combine infused gin, lemon, syrup, purée, and cream. Refrigerate up to 24 hours in a sealed container. Do not batch with egg white or bitters.
- **To serve:** Measure 3¾ oz batched base per drink, add egg white and bitters, dry-shake, wet-shake, fine-strain over fresh ice, top with soda.

### Scaling
- **Linear scaling:** Multiply all base ingredients (except egg white, bitters, and soda) by the number of servings.
- **Egg white:** One egg white serves 2–3 drinks; for larger batches, use 1 egg white per 3 servings and dry-shake the full batch, then portion and wet-shake individually.
- **Bitters:** Scale linearly (3 drops per serving).
- **Soda:** Always top per glass to preserve carbonation.
