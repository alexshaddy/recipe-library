# Tortuga

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Handwritten guest-check recipe from the Mockingbird Bar scan set (assets/loose_notes_scans/brw849e563cc961_000405.jpg). "Tortuga" (Spanish for "turtle") evokes the drink's signature green-tinged strawberry-cream color. The structure is a strawberry-cream sour: base spirit, lemon, lime, strawberry purée, cream, egg white, bitters.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 1 cocktail*

- [ ] `2 ounces` — **strawberry-infused blanco tequila** (house-infused; resolved from "[UNCLEAR], likely strawberry or another red fruit spirit" — tequila fits the "Tortuga" name and pairs with strawberry/lime)
- [ ] `3/4 ounce` — fresh lemon juice
- [ ] `1/2 ounce` — fresh lime juice
- [ ] `3/4 ounce` — **strawberry purée** (fresh or frozen strawberries blended and fine-strained; resolved from "berry component [UNCLEAR]")
- [ ] `1/2 ounce` — **heavy cream** (36–40%; resolved from "cream component or liqueur [UNCLEAR]" — cream provides body and stabilizes foam; a liqueur would add unwanted sweetness)
- [ ] `1` — egg white (or 15 ml aquafaba for vegan)
- [ ] `3 drops` — **Angostura bitters** (resolved from "aromatic bitters or similar [UNCLEAR]" — classic for cream sours)
- [ ] Ice — for shaking (cubes) and serving (one large cube in rocks glass, or up in coupe)
- [ ] **Garnish:** strawberry half on rim, light grate of fresh nutmeg over foam

---

## Instructions

1. **Dry-shake the foam.** Add strawberry-infused blanco tequila, lemon juice, lime juice, strawberry purée, heavy cream, egg white, and Angostura bitters to a shaker *without ice*. Shake vigorously for 15–20 seconds to emulsify the egg white and cream.
2. **Wet-shake to chill.** Add ice cubes and shake hard for 10–12 seconds until the tin is frosted.
3. **Fine-strain.** Double-strain (fine strainer + hawthorne) into a chilled coupe (served up) or a rocks glass over one large cube.
4. **Garnish.** Rest a strawberry half on the rim; lightly grate fresh nutmeg over the foam cap.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Base spirit resolution:** The original "[UNCLEAR], likely strawberry or another red fruit spirit" paired with the name *Tortuga* (Spanish for turtle) strongly suggests a strawberry-infused *blanco tequila*. Tequila's vegetal agave notes complement strawberry and lime; the green tint of blanco tequila + red strawberry purée creates the namesake "turtle" color. A strawberry-infused gin or vodka would work but lose the thematic link.
- **Sweetener note:** No separate sweetener appears in the scan, but strawberry purée provides sufficient sweetness (~8–10° Brix). If using underripe berries, add ¼ oz rich simple syrup.
- **Cream resolution:** "Cream component or liqueur [UNCLEAR]" — cream is correct for a sour structure; a cream liqueur (e.g., Baileys) would overwhelm the tequila and add cloying sweetness. Heavy cream (36–40%) stabilizes the egg-white foam.
- **Citrus balance:** Lemon (¾ oz) + lime (½ oz) gives a brighter, more complex acidity than lemon alone, lifting the strawberry-cream richness.
- **Bitters resolution:** Three drops Angostura is the standard aromatic garnish/flavor bridge for cream sours; it sits on the foam and aromatizes each sip.
- **Method rationale:** Dry-shake → wet-shake is mandatory for egg-white drinks. Double-straining ensures a silky texture. Serving up in a coupe showcases the foam; over a large cube in a rocks glass slows dilution for slower sipping.

### Variations
- **Tortuga Verde:** Use strawberry-infused mezcal (½ oz mezcal + 1.5 oz tequila) for a smoky-turtle variant.
- **Frozen Tortuga:** Blend all ingredients (except bitters) with 1 cup crushed ice; pour into a rocks glass; grate nutmeg on top.
- **Nondairy / Vegan:** Replace heavy cream with ½ oz coconut cream; swap egg white for 15 ml aquafaba. Dry-shake longer (25 sec).

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Batch base (no egg white, no bitters):** Combine infused tequila, citrus, purée, and cream. Refrigerate up to 24 hours in a sealed container. Do not batch with egg white.
- **To serve:** Measure 3¾ oz batched base per drink, add egg white and bitters, dry-shake, wet-shake, double-strain.

### Scaling
- **Linear scaling:** Multiply all base ingredients (except egg white and bitters) by the number of servings.
- **Egg white:** One egg white serves 2–3 drinks; for larger batches, use 1 egg white per 3 servings and dry-shake the full batch, then portion and wet-shake individually.
- **Bitters:** Scale linearly (3 drops per serving).
