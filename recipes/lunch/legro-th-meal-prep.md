# Legro-th Meal Prep

> [!NOTE]
> Batch-prep session with three components: bacon fried rice, pasta sauce, and sautéed vegetables. Cook each component separately, then portion for the week. Each recipe makes enough for ~3 lunch portions.

---

## Component 1: Bacon Fried Rice

A straightforward fried rice built on rendered bacon fat. Good for using leftover rice.

### Ingredients

- [ ] `2` — carrots, small dice
- [ ] `1` — onion, small dice
- [ ] `3 cups` — cooked rice (day-old preferred)
- [ ] `1 cup` — frozen peas
- [ ] `3` — eggs, lightly beaten
- [ ] `4 strips` — bacon, cut into ½-inch pieces
- [ ] `2 tablespoons` — soy sauce
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — rice wine vinegar
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — sugar
- [ ] `2` — scallions, sliced (for garnish)

### Instructions

1. **Render the bacon.** Cook bacon pieces in a large skillet or wok over medium heat until crispy, 5–7 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the fat in the pan.

2. **Sauté the aromatics.** Add carrots and onion to the bacon fat. Cook over medium-high heat until softened and beginning to brown, 4–5 minutes.

3. **Scramble the eggs.** Push vegetables to one side of the pan. Pour beaten eggs into the empty side and scramble until just set, about 1 minute. Fold into the vegetables.

4. **Finish the rice.** Add the cooked rice, peas, soy sauce, rice wine vinegar, and sugar. Toss everything together, breaking up any clumps of rice. Cook until the rice is heated through and slightly crispy in spots, 3–4 minutes. Return the bacon to the pan, toss once more, and serve garnished with scallions.

## Component 2: Pasta Sauce (Meat Sauce)

A simple weeknight-style sauce built from ground beef and canned tomatoes. Not a long-simmer ragu — quick enough for a prep session.

### Ingredients

- [ ] `1 pound` — ground beef
- [ ] `1` — onion, small dice
- [ ] `1` — carrot, small dice
- [ ] `2 cloves` — garlic, minced
- [ ] `1 can (14 oz)` — crushed tomatoes
- [ ] `2 tablespoons` — tomato paste
- [ ] `¼ cup` — red wine
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — red wine vinegar
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — sugar
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — salt
- [ ] `½ teaspoon` — black pepper
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — olive oil

### Instructions

1. **Brown the beef.** Heat olive oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add ground beef and cook, breaking it apart with a spoon, until browned and no longer pink, 6–8 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.

2. **Sauté the vegetables.** Add onion and carrot to the pan. Cook over medium heat until softened, 5–6 minutes. Add garlic and cook until fragrant, 30 seconds.

3. **Build the sauce.** Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1 minute to deepen its flavor. Deglaze the pan with red wine, scraping up any browned bits. Add crushed tomatoes, red wine vinegar, sugar, salt, pepper, and the browned beef. Stir to combine.

4. **Simmer.** Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 15–20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly. Taste and adjust salt. Serve over pasta or refrigerate for the week.

## Component 3: Sautéed Mushrooms and Vegetables

A quick vegetable side that works alongside the fried rice, pasta, or as a standalone lunch addition.

### Ingredients

- [ ] `1` — onion, sliced
- [ ] `1` — carrot, julienned or thinly sliced on the bias
- [ ] `1` — zucchini, halved lengthwise and sliced into half-moons
- [ ] `2 stalks` — celery, sliced
- [ ] `8 oz` — mushrooms (cremini or button), sliced
- [ ] `2 tablespoons` — butter or olive oil
- [ ] `½ teaspoon` — salt
- [ ] `¼ teaspoon` — black pepper
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

### Instructions

1. **Sauté the aromatics.** Heat butter or oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and carrot and cook until beginning to soften, 3–4 minutes.

2. **Cook the mushrooms.** Add mushrooms and celery to the pan. Cook without stirring too often so the mushrooms get some color, 5–6 minutes, until they have released their liquid and started to brown.

3. **Finish with zucchini.** Add zucchini, salt, and pepper. Toss and cook until the zucchini is just tender but not mushy, 2–3 minutes more. Stir in parsley if using. Serve warm or at room temperature.

---

## Batch Assembly

1. **Portion.** Divide each component into individual containers. Fried rice and sautéed vegetables go into lunch boxes; pasta sauce can be stored separately and reheated with fresh pasta.

2. **Label and refrigerate.** All three components keep in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. The pasta sauce also freezes well for up to 2 months.

3. **Reheat.** Microwave or reheat in a skillet with a splash of water to revive the texture. The fried rice is best re-crisped in a hot pan.
