# Open-Face Mushroom Sammie

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Best-effort transcription from notebook page 18. A simple, satisfying open-face sandwich with garlicky sautéed mushrooms piled high on crisp toast.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 2 sammies*

- [ ] `2 thick slices` — hearty bread (sourdough or country loaf)
- [ ] `2 cups` — mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, or button), sliced
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — butter or olive oil
- [ ] `1 clove` — garlic, minced
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — fresh thyme leaves (or 1/2 tsp dried thyme)
- [ ] `to taste` — salt and freshly ground black pepper
- [ ] *Optional: splash of sherry or white wine for deglazing*
- [ ] *Optional garnish: chopped parsley, grated Parmesan, or a drizzle of truffle oil*

---

## Instructions

1. **Toast the Bread.** Toast or grill the bread slices until golden brown and crisp on both sides. The bread should be sturdy enough to support the topping without getting soggy. Set aside on serving plates.

2. **Heat the Pan.** Place a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add 1 tablespoon butter or olive oil and let it heat until the butter foams or the oil shimmers (~1 min).

3. **Sear the Mushrooms.** Add the sliced mushrooms to the hot pan in a single layer. Let them sit undisturbed until the bottoms are deeply browned and they release easily from the pan (~3–4 min). You should hear a steady sizzle and smell a rich, earthy aroma. Resist the urge to stir — crowding or moving them too soon will steam instead of sear.

4. **Add Aromatics.** Once the mushrooms are browned, stir and add the minced garlic and thyme. Cook, stirring frequently, until the garlic is fragrant and just beginning to turn golden (~1 min). Season generously with salt and pepper.

5. **Deglaze (Optional).** If using, add a splash of sherry or white wine to the pan and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom. Let it bubble and reduce until nearly evaporated (~30 sec).

6. **Assemble.** Pile the hot mushrooms generously onto the toasted bread. Garnish with chopped parsley, grated Parmesan, or a drizzle of truffle oil if desired. Serve immediately while the mushrooms are hot and the toast is crisp.

---

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- Letting the mushrooms sear undisturbed is the key to deep, caramelized flavor — stirring too early releases water and steams them.
- Use a mix of mushroom varieties (cremini, shiitake, oyster) for more complex flavor and texture.

### Variations
- **Creamy Mushroom Sammie:** After cooking the mushrooms, stir in 2 tablespoons of crème fraîche or sour cream off heat, then pile onto toast.
- **Vegan Mushroom Sammie:** Use olive oil instead of butter and skip the Parmesan; add a sprinkle of nutritional yeast for a cheesy note.

### Make-Ahead
- Mushrooms are best cooked just before serving; they lose their crisp edges when reheated.
- Bread can be toasted ahead and kept warm in a low oven (200°F) for up to 15 minutes.

### Storage
- Best assembled and eaten immediately. Leftover mushrooms can be refrigerated for up to 2 days and reheated in a hot skillet, though the texture will soften.

### Scaling
- Make the mushroom topping in small batches so it browns instead of steaming.
- For more servings, cook mushrooms in batches and keep warm, then assemble all at once.
