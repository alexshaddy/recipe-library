# Bread Basics — Golden Ratio (5:3 Flour:Water)

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Notebook page 25: "Bread ratio 5:3 flour:water." This is a 60% hydration artisan straight dough. The [TO VERIFY] on yeast is resolved below: instant dry yeast at 0.7% flour weight for same-day straight dough; 0.1% for 12–18 hr cold retard. Salt fixed at 2% flour weight. No sugar or fat in this lean formula — the notebook's "0.5 part sugar, 0.25 part fat" reflects an enriched variant; the lean golden ratio is simply flour, water, salt, yeast.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 1 loaf (≈900 g baked weight)*

### By Weight (Golden Ratio Formula)
- [ ] `500 g` (3½ cups) **bread flour** (11.5–13% protein; e.g., King Arthur Bread Flour) — **100%**
- [ ] `300 g` (1¼ cups + 1 tbsp) **water**, 75–80°F (24–27°C) — **60%**
- [ ] `10 g` (2 tsp) **fine sea salt** — **2%**
- [ ] `3.5 g` (1 tsp) **instant dry yeast** — **0.7%** (same-day) *or* `0.5 g` (⅛ tsp) — **0.1%** (overnight cold retard)

### Optional Enrichment (Notebook Variant)
- [ ] `25 g` (2 tbsp) **granulated sugar** — **5%** (adds browning, softens crumb)
- [ ] `12 g` (1 tbsp) **olive oil or butter** — **2.5%** (improves keeping quality)

---

## Instructions

1. **Weigh and combine (mix).** In a large bowl, whisk flour and salt to distribute. In a separate container, whisk water and yeast until dissolved (instant yeast needs no bloom). Make a well in the flour; pour in water-yeast mixture. Using a stiff spatula or wet hand, mix 2 minutes until no dry flour remains — shaggy, sticky mass. *Sensory: dough clings to fingers, no flour pockets.*
2. **Autolyse.** Cover bowl with damp towel or lid. Rest 30 minutes at room temp (72–76°F / 22–24°C). *No kneading yet — this hydrates gluten and starts enzymatic activity. Dough relaxes visibly.*
3. **Develop gluten (mix/knead).** Turn dough onto unfloured surface. Perform **slap-and-fold** (or **stretch-and-fold in bowl**) 4 minutes: lift dough, slap down, fold over, rotate 90°, repeat. *Sensory: dough transforms from sticky/shaggy to smooth, elastic, slightly tacky — passes windowpane test (stretch thin enough to read text through without tearing).*
4. **Bulk fermentation (1st rise) with stretch & folds.** Return dough to bowl, cover. At 30 min, 60 min, and 90 min marks (3 folds total): wet hand, grab one side, stretch up, fold to center; rotate bowl 90°, repeat 4× per set. *Each fold tightens structure; dough grows 30–50% volume, shows bubbles at edges, feels alive/pillowy.* Total bulk: **2–2.5 hours** at 74–76°F (23–24°C). *If kitchen cold (<70°F), extend 30–60 min.*
5. **Pre-shape.** Turn dough onto lightly floured bench. Gently flatten to rectangle. Fold top third down, bottom third up (letter fold). Round into loose boule by tucking edges under with bench scraper. Bench rest 20 min, uncovered (or lightly covered if dry air). *Sensory: dough relaxes, spreads slightly, skin forms.*
6. **Final shape (boule).** Flour proofing basket (banneton) generously with rice flour or 50/50 AP/rice flour. Lightly flour dough top. Invert so smooth side down. Fold 4 "envelope" folds: top to center, bottom to center, left to center, right to center. Flip seam-side up into basket. Pinch seams tight. *Sensory: taut skin, defined shape, holds contour.*
7. **Cold retard (optional, preferred) OR room-temp proof.** **Method A (Retard):** Cover basket with bag; refrigerate 12–18 hours. Next morning, bake straight from cold. **Method B (Same-day):** Cover; proof at 74–76°F 1–1.5 hours until puffed 50% and finger-poke dent springs back slowly (½ in / 1 cm). *Don't overproof — dent should not stay.*
8. **Preheat with steam vessel.** Place Dutch oven (lid on) in oven; preheat 500°F (260°C) **45 minutes minimum** — thermal mass must saturate. *If no Dutch oven: preheat baking steel/stone at 500°F 1 hr; set a metal pan on lower rack for boiling water steam.*
9. **Score and bake.** Turn dough onto parchment. Score boldly with lame: ¼-inch deep, 45° angle, one long "ear" cut or cross. Slide into screaming-hot Dutch oven; cover. Bake **20 min covered** (steam sets oven spring). Reduce to 450°F (230°C); remove lid. Bake **20–25 min uncovered** until deep mahogany, internal temp 208–210°F (98–99°C). *Sensory: crust crackles on cooling (the "song"), loaf feels light for its size.*
10. **Cool completely.** Transfer to wire rack. **Do not cut for 2 hours minimum** (4 hours ideal). Cutting hot = gummy crumb. Starch retrogradation finishes as it cools.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Yeast resolution:** The notebook's "[TO VERIFY] yeast or leavening" is resolved as **instant dry yeast at 0.7% flour weight** for same-day bake (3.5 g / 500 g flour). For superior flavor, use **0.1% (0.5 g)** with 12–18 hour cold retard — the long fermentation develops organic acids and complexity impossible in 3 hours. Do not exceed 1% yeast — it blows past fermentation flavor.
- **60% hydration = forgiving window.** This is not a high-hydration dough (75%+). It handles easily, shapes cleanly, and produces an open-but-structured crumb with thin, crisp crust. Great for learning hand techniques.
- **Salt = 2% non-negotiable.** Controls fermentation rate, strengthens gluten, flavors crumb. Under-salted dough ferments too fast, collapses, tastes flat. Weigh it.
- **Steam is the crust.** Dutch oven traps dough's own steam for the first 20 min — this delays crust set, allows maximum oven spring. No Dutch oven? 1 cup boiling water in preheated pan at bake start + spray oven walls 3× in first 5 min.
- **Don't rush the cool.** The crumb sets as steam escapes. Cutting at 1 hour = dense, gummy center. 2 hours = acceptable. 4 hours = textbook.

### Variations
- **Whole Wheat Blend:** Substitute 100 g (20%) bread flour with whole wheat flour. Add 10 g extra water (whole wheat absorbs more). Autolyse 1 hour.
- **Semolina Crust:** Dust proofing basket with fine semolina instead of rice flour — gives golden, sandy crust texture.
- **Herbed Loaf:** Fold 15 g chopped fresh rosemary + 10 g lemon zest into dough at 1st stretch-and-fold.
- **Enriched "Notebook Variant":** Add 25 g sugar + 12 g olive oil with water (step 1). Reduce bake temp to 425°F (220°C) after lid off — sugar browns faster. Softer crust, tighter crumb, keeps 4 days.
- **Sourdough Conversion:** Replace 100 g flour + 60 g water + 0.5 g yeast with 160 g active levain (100% hydration). Bulk ferment 4–5 hours at 76°F. Same ratio, wild yeast.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Dough retard:** Shaped loaf in basket, bagged, refrigerates 12–36 hours. Bake straight from cold — no warm-up needed.
- **Baked loaf:** Best day-of. Stores 3 days at room temp in paper bag (cut side down on board). Freezes 3 months — slice before freezing; toast from frozen.
- **Stale bread uses:** Day 3 → croutons, breadcrumbs, panzanella, French toast, bread pudding.

### Scaling
- **Golden Ratio math:** Pick flour weight → water = 60% flour, salt = 2%, yeast = 0.1–0.7%.
  - Small (500 g flour): 300 g water, 10 g salt, 0.5–3.5 g yeast → 1 loaf
  - Medium (1 kg flour): 600 g water, 20 g salt, 1–7 g yeast → 2 loaves
  - Large (2 kg flour): 1.2 kg water, 40 g salt, 2–14 g yeast → 4 loaves (mix in 2 batches)
- **Baking multiples:** Dutch oven holds 1 loaf. For 2+, bake sequentially (reheat Dutch oven 10 min between) or use baking steel + steam pan.
- **Hydration tweaks:** 60% is the "golden" baseline. +5% (65%) = more open crumb, stickier dough. -5% (55%) = tighter crumb, easier handling.
