# Broccoli (Sicilian Agrodolce)

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Expanded from 6.21 pop-up dinner notebook (pages 21–22). The original note described a simple garlic-olive oil broccoli sauté; Addendum III specifies the Sicilian agrodolce treatment — caramelized sugar deglazed with vinegar, studded with raisins and pine nuts — a classic preparation from Palermo.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 4 portions*

- [ ] `2` heads (≈2 lb / 900 g) broccoli, cut into 1½-inch florets (stems peeled and sliced)
- [ ] `3 tbsp` extra-virgin olive oil, divided
- [ ] `3 cloves` garlic, thinly sliced
- [ ] `½ tsp` red pepper flakes (peperoncino)
- [ ] `2 tbsp` granulated sugar
- [ ] `2 tbsp` red wine vinegar (or sherry vinegar)
- [ ] `¼ cup` golden raisins (sultanas)
- [ ] `¼ cup` pine nuts (toasted)
- [ ] `1 tsp` fine sea salt, plus more for blanching water
- [ ] `¼ tsp` freshly ground black pepper
- [ ] `1 tbsp` fresh mint, torn (optional, for garnish)
- [ ] `½ lemon`, zest only (optional, for brightness)

---

## Instructions

1. **Blanch the broccoli.** Bring a large pot of heavily salted water (1 tbsp salt per quart) to a rolling boil. Add broccoli florets and stem pieces; cook 90 seconds — just until bright green and barely yielding to a knife tip. Drain immediately in a colander; do not shock in ice water (surface moisture helps the glaze adhere).
2. **Toast the pine nuts.** Wipe out a 10-inch skillet; add pine nuts over medium heat. Toast 60–90 seconds, shaking constantly, until golden and fragrant. Transfer to a small bowl; set aside.
3. **Sauté the aromatics.** Return skillet to medium heat; add 2 tbsp olive oil and sliced garlic. Cook 30–40 seconds until garlic edges turn pale gold — do not brown. Stir in red pepper flakes; cook 10 seconds until oil is fragrant and red-tinged.
4. **Caramelize the sugar.** Push garlic to the sides; sprinkle sugar into the center of the skillet. Let melt undisturbed 1 minute, then swirl gently until deep amber (320–330°F / 160–165°C), 2–3 minutes total. Smell: toasted caramel, not burnt.
5. **Deglaze with vinegar.** Carefully pour in vinegar — it will sputter violently. Stand back; whisk vigorously with a heatproof spatula until the caramel dissolves into a glossy syrup, 30 seconds. The mixture will steam and reduce by about half.
6. **Build the agrodolce.** Stir in raisins; simmer 30 seconds until they plump. Return toasted pine nuts to the pan. Taste: balance should be sweet-sour with a whisper of heat — adjust with a pinch of salt or splash of vinegar if needed.
7. **Coat the broccoli.** Add blanched broccoli to the skillet; toss continuously over medium-high heat 2 minutes until every floret is lacquered and heated through. The glaze will cling, not pool.
8. **Season and serve.** Finish with remaining 1 tbsp olive oil, salt, pepper, lemon zest, and torn mint (if using). Transfer to a warm platter; serve immediately while the glaze is sticky and the broccoli still has bite.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Caramel safety:** Sugar goes from amber to burnt in seconds. Have the vinegar measured and ready *before* you start the caramel. If it burns, discard and start over — bitter caramel ruins the dish.
- **Blanch, don't steam:** Boiling water sets the chlorophyll (bright green) and pre-cooks the dense stems so the quick glaze step finishes them without mush. Ice-water shocking would wash off surface starch that helps the glaze cling.
- **Vinegar choice:** Red wine vinegar gives a fruity acidity; sherry vinegar adds nutty depth. Avoid balsamic — too sweet and dark. For a cleaner profile, use white wine vinegar.
- **Texture window:** Serve within 5 minutes of glazing. The agrodolce softens broccoli fast; leftovers reheat poorly (become mushy). If making ahead, keep blanched broccoli and agrodolce separate; combine and heat at service.

### Variations
- **Cauliflower Agrodolce:** Substitute cauliflower florets; blanch 2 minutes (denser). Add 1 tsp fennel seed with the garlic for anise note.
- **Spicy-Sweet with Calabrian Chile:** Swap red pepper flakes for 1 tsp Calabrian chile paste (in oil); omit pine nuts, add 2 tbsp toasted breadcrumbs for crunch.
- **Anchovy-Enhanced:** Add 2 oil-packed anchovy fillets with the garlic; mash into the oil before caramelizing sugar. Omit extra salt.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Blanched broccoli:** Up to 4 hours at room temperature (not refrigerated — cold broccoli won't glaze properly).
- **Agrodolce base (sugar + vinegar + raisins + pine nuts):** Up to 3 days refrigerated in a jar. Reheat gently before tossing with broccoli.
- **Full dish:** Do not make ahead. Reheated agrodolce broccoli loses texture. For service, blanch broccoli during prep; make agrodolce to order (5 min).

### Scaling
- **2 servings:** Halve all ingredients; use 8-inch skillet. Blanch 1 head broccoli.
- **8 servings:** Double all; blanch in 2 batches. Make agrodolce in 12-inch skillet or 2×10-inch skillets. Pine nuts: toast in two batches.
- **Large event (20+):** Blanch broccoli day-of; hold at room temp. Make agrodolce base (sugar, vinegar, raisins, pine nuts) morning of; reheat and toss broccoli in batches 15 min before service.
