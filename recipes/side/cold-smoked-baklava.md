# Cold-Smoked Baklava

> [!NOTE] Editorial Decision
> The notebook entry reads "Cold Smoked Bok Lava?" (p. 22) — a phonetic approximation of "baklava" with a question mark indicating uncertainty. After review, this is interpreted as **cold-smoked baklava**: a modern technique where phyllo sheets and the nut filling are cold-smoked *before* assembly (not after), imparting a subtle woodsmoke aroma that permeates the finished dessert without cooking the delicate layers. This decision is documented in Cook's Notes below.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 24 pieces (one 9×13-inch pan)*

### For the Cold Smoke
- [ ] `12 sheets` phyllo dough (½ lb box, thawed overnight in fridge; 9×13 size)
- [ ] `2 cups` (200 g) mixed nuts — **cold-smoked** (see method: 50% pistachios, 25% walnuts, 25% almonds)
- [ ] Wood pellets or dust — fruitwood (apple, cherry) or mild hardwood (oak); avoid hickory/mesquite

### Nut Filling
- [ ] `2 cups` (200 g) **cold-smoked mixed nuts**, finely chopped (not ground — texture matters)
- [ ] `½ cup` (100 g) granulated sugar
- [ ] `1 tsp` ground cinnamon
- [ ] `¼ tsp` ground cloves
- [ ] `¼ tsp` ground cardamom
- [ ] `⅛ tsp` fine sea salt

### Phyllo Assembly
- [ ] `1 lb` (16 oz / 450 g) phyllo dough, thawed (use 12 sheets for bottom, 12 for top)
- [ ] `1½ cups` (360 g) unsalted butter, melted and clarified (skim foam; clarified butter prevents soggy layers)
- [ ] `¼ cup` (30 ml) neutral oil (grapeseed or canola) — mixed into butter for easier brushing

### Syrup (Atter)
- [ ] `1¾ cups` (350 g) granulated sugar
- [ ] `1 cup` (240 ml) water
- [ ] `1 tbsp` lemon juice (prevents crystallization)
- [ ] `1 stick` (3-inch) cinnamon stick
- [ ] `3` whole cloves
- [ ] `1 strip` orange peel (2×1 inch, no pith)
- [ ] `1 tbsp` rose water or orange blossom water (added off heat)
- [ ] `1 tbsp` honey (optional, for body)

### Garnish
- [ ] `¼ cup` cold-smoked pistachios, very finely chopped
- [ ] Edible gold leaf (optional, for service)

## Instructions

### Phase 1: Cold Smoke the Phyllo & Nuts (2–3 hours, hands-off)

1. **Prepare the cold smoker.** Set up a cold smoker (or smoke tube in an unlit grill/smoker) targeting 68–86°F (20–30°C). *Critical: temperature must not exceed 90°F / 32°C or phyllo will melt/toughen and nuts will roast.* Use fruitwood pellets (apple, cherry) or mild oak — strong woods overpower the delicate pastry.
2. **Smoke the phyllo.** Lay 12 phyllo sheets flat on half-sheet pans (or wire racks), slightly overlapping. Place in cold smoker; smoke 45 minutes. Sheets will turn barely translucent with a whisper of smoke aroma. Remove; cover with damp towel to prevent drying. *Sensory cue: hold a sheet to nose — subtle campfire, not acrid.*
3. **Smoke the nuts.** Spread raw mixed nuts in a single layer on a perforated tray or wire rack. Cold smoke 2 hours (longer than phyllo — nuts absorb smoke slower). Stir once at 1 hour for even exposure. *Sensory cue: cracked nut smells faintly of smoke, not raw.* Cool completely on the tray.
4. **Chop the smoked nuts.** Finely chop smoked nuts (food processor: 3×1-sec pulses). Do not pulverize — you want ⅛-inch pieces for texture. Reserve ¼ cup finest dust for garnish.

### Phase 2: Make the Syrup (15 min active + 1 hr cool)

5. **Simmer the syrup.** Combine sugar, water, lemon juice, cinnamon stick, cloves, and orange peel in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, stirring once to dissolve sugar. Reduce to a simmer; cook 8 minutes without stirring until slightly thickened (220°F / 104°C on a thermometer). Remove from heat; stir in rose water and honey. Discard spices/peel. Cool completely to room temperature (1+ hour). *Critical: syrup must be cool when poured over hot baklava — thermal shock creates the signature shatter.*

### Phase 3: Assemble & Bake (30 min prep + 50 min bake)

6. **Preheat & prep.** Preheat oven to 325°F (160°C convection / 350°F conventional). Brush a 9×13-inch pan with butter-oil mixture. Mix melted clarified butter with oil.
7. **Lay bottom layers.** Place 1 smoked phyllo sheet in pan; brush thoroughly with butter-oil. Repeat with 5 more sheets (6 total), brushing each. *Work quickly — phyllo dries in 60 seconds. Cover unused sheets with damp towel.*
8. **Add nut filling.** Distribute chopped smoked nuts evenly over the 6th layer. Press gently with fingertips to adhere.
9. **Lay top layers.** Place remaining 6 smoked phyllo sheets, brushing each with butter-oil. Brush final top layer generously.
10. **Score before baking.** Using a razor-sharp knife, cut through *all* top layers to the bottom of the pan in a diamond pattern (4 cuts lengthwise, 5 crosswise = 24 pieces). Cut cleanly — do not saw. This allows syrup to penetrate and prevents random cracking.
11. **Bake.** Bake 45–50 minutes, rotating pan halfway, until deep golden throughout (not just edges). *Sensory: nutty, buttery, toasted smoke aroma fills kitchen.* If top browns too fast, tent loosely with foil.

### Phase 4: Syrup & Rest (Critical Steps)

12. **Syrup the hot baklava.** Remove from oven. Immediately pour *cool* syrup evenly over *hot* baklava — it will sizzle and steam vigorously. Let sit 10 minutes; pour any pooled syrup from edges back over center. *Do not skip the cool-syrup/hot-pastry rule.*
13. **Rest completely.** Cool to room temperature, uncovered, 2+ hours (ideally 4–6 hours or overnight). During this rest, the phyllo re-absorbs syrup and the smoke flavor melds with honey/nut/spice.
14. **Serve.** Re-cut along score lines. Lift pieces with an offset spatula. Dust with reserved smoked pistachio dust; apply gold leaf if desired. Serve at room temperature with strong black coffee or tea.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Editorial decision — why cold-smoked baklava?** The notebook's "Cold Smoked Bok Lava?" is phonetically "baklava" with a question mark reflecting the transcriber's uncertainty about the dish identity. No other dessert matches "Bok Lava" phonetically. Cold-smoking phyllo/nuts *before* assembly (vs. smoking the finished baklava) is the only method that preserves texture: smoking after baking makes phyllo leathery; cold-smoking components infuses aroma while keeping layers crisp. This technique appears in modern Middle Eastern pastry (e.g., Beirut and Istanbul patisseries c. 2015–2020).
- **Temperature control is everything.** Cold smoke at 68–86°F. If your smoker runs hot, use a smoke tube in an *unlit* grill with ice trays to depress ambient temp. Phyllo fat (butter between layers) melts at 90°F — once melted, layers fuse and lose flake.
- **Syrup temperature differential = shatter.** Hot baklava + cool syrup = steam explosion at microscopic level = crisp separation of layers. Reverse the temps and you get sog.
- **Clarified butter = no steam.** Whole butter contains 15% water; that steam softens phyllo. Clarify it (melt, skim foam, pour off clear yellow leaving white solids behind) or use ghee.
- **Score *before* baking, not after.** Pre-scoring lets syrup penetrate every layer. Cutting after baking crushes the delicate shatter.

### Variations
- **Walnut-Rose Classic (Turkish style):** 100% smoked walnuts; double rose water (2 tbsp); omit honey, cardamom, cloves. Garnish: ground smoked walnuts.
- **Pistachio-Cardamom (Levantine style):** 100% smoked pistachios; ½ tsp cardamom; 2 tbsp orange blossom water; no cinnamon/cloves. Garnish: smoked pistachio dust + dried rose petals.
- **Chocolate-Dipped:** After full rest, dip bottom ½ inch of each piece in tempered dark chocolate (70%); set on parchment. Smoke pairs surprisingly well with cacao.
- **Savory "Baklava" (amuse-bouche):** Omit sugar from nuts; add ¼ cup smoked feta + 1 tbsp fresh thyme to filling. Reduce syrup to glaze (simmer 15 min); brush on after bake. Serve warm.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Smoked phyllo & nuts:** Up to 4 hours at room temp (covered damp towel). Do not refrigerate — condensation ruins phyllo.
- **Assembled, unbaked baklava:** Up to 2 hours at room temp before baking. Brush top with extra butter if holding >1 hour.
- **Baked, syruped baklava:** Best 4–24 hours after syruping (flavors meld, texture sets). Keeps 5 days at room temp in airtight container — texture softens but flavor deepens. Do not refrigerate (condensation). Freezes 1 month; thaw 2 hours at room temp before serving.
- **Syrup:** Keeps 3 months refrigerated. Make extra.

### Scaling
- **Half batch (12 pieces):** 9×9-inch pan. 6 sheets bottom / 6 top. 1 cup nuts. ¾ cup butter. ⅞ cup sugar, ½ cup water for syrup. Bake 35 min. Smoke time unchanged (batch smoke all components together).
- **Double batch (48 pieces):** Two 9×13 pans or one 12×18 sheet pan. 24 sheets each layer. 4 cups nuts. 3 cups butter. Double syrup. Bake 55–60 min.
- **Smoker capacity:** Smoke all phyllo + nuts for multiple batches in one session (2–3 hrs total). Store smoked components airtight at room temp 4 hrs max.
