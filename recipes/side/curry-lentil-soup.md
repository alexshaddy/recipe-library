# Curry Lentil Soup

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Best-effort transcription from notebook page 17. A warming, aromatic lentil soup with bloomed curry spices and a bright acidic finish.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 4 bowls*

- [ ] `1 cup` — lentils (red, green, or brown), rinsed and picked over
- [ ] `1` — onion, diced
- [ ] `2 cloves` — garlic, minced
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — curry powder
- [ ] `4 cups` — stock or water
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — neutral oil
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — salt, or to taste
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — lemon juice or apple cider vinegar (for finishing)
- [ ] *Optional: 1 tablespoon olive oil or coconut milk for richness*
- [ ] *Optional garnishes: chopped cilantro, yogurt, chili oil*

---

## Instructions

1. **Bloom the Curry Powder.** Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the curry powder and stir constantly until fragrant and slightly darkened (~30–60 sec), taking care not to burn. The mixture should smell toasty and aromatic.

2. **Sweat the Aromatics.** Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the onion becomes translucent and soft (~5–7 min). The mixture should be sweet-smelling and the onions should be glossy.

3. **Add Lentils and Liquid.** Stir in 1 cup of lentils (rinsed and picked over) and 4 cups of stock or water. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to maintain a simmer.

4. **Simmer Until Tender.** Partially cover the pot and let the soup simmer until the lentils are soft and starting to break apart (~25–35 min). Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. The lentils should be tender but not mushy.

5. **Blend for Creaminess (Optional).** For a smoother texture, use an immersion blender to puree part or all of the soup directly in the pot. Alternatively, transfer half of the soup to a countertop blender, blend until smooth, and return to the pot. Blend to your desired consistency.

6. **Finish with Acid and Fat.** Remove the pot from heat. Stir in 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or apple cider vinegar to brighten the flavors, and optionally swirl in 1 tablespoon of olive oil or coconut milk for richness. Adjust salt to taste.

7. **Garnish and Serve.** Ladle the soup into bowls. Top with optional garnishes such as chopped cilantro, a dollop of yogurt, or a drizzle of chili oil. Serve hot.

---

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- Blooming the curry powder in oil first unlocks its essential oils and prevents a raw, bitter taste.
- If using red lentils, they will break down more quickly and create a naturally thicker soup; green or brown lentils hold their shape better.

### Variations
- **Coconut Curry Lentil Soup:** Replace half the stock with coconut milk and add a teaspoon of grated ginger with the aromatics for a Thai-inspired twist.

### Make-Ahead
- The soup keeps refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 4 days.
- Freeze in portion-sized containers for up to 3 months; thaw and reheat gently, adding a splash of water or stock if needed.

### Storage
- Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Lentils thicken as they sit, so loosen with stock when reheating.
- Freeze for up to 3 months; thaw and reheat gently.

### Scaling
- The recipe scales linearly; maintain the ratio of 1 cup lentils to 4 cups liquid.
- When doubling, use a larger pot to avoid overcrowding and ensure even simmering.
