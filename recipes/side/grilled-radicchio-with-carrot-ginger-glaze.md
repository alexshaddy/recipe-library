# Grilled Radicchio with Carrot-Ginger Glaze

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Expanded from Chef's Recipe Notebook page 24 (6.21 pop-up dinner notebook). The original sketch showed grilled radicchio quarters with a bright carrot-ginger glaze, nori, sesame, and "chanterelle powder?" — the question mark resolved as dried chanterelle mushroom powder, an umami finishing dust that bridges the sweet glaze and bitter radicchio.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 4 portions*

### Carrot-Ginger Glaze
- [ ] `3` large carrots (≈250 g), peeled and rough-chopped
- [ ] `1½ tbsp` fresh ginger, peeled and grated (≈15 g)
- [ ] `2 tbsp` granulated sugar
- [ ] `2 tbsp` rice wine vinegar
- [ ] `1 tbsp` white wine vinegar
- [ ] `1 tsp` toasted sesame oil
- [ ] `¼ tsp` fine sea salt
- [ ] `2 tbsp` water (only if needed for blender)

### Radicchio
- [ ] `2` heads radicchio di Chioggia (each ~½ lb / 225 g), core trimmed, cut into 4 wedges through the core (8 wedges total) — core holds leaves together on grill
- [ ] `2 tbsp` extra-virgin olive oil
- [ ] `1 tsp` flaky sea salt (Maldon or similar)
- [ ] `¼ tsp` freshly ground black pepper

### Garnish
- [ ] `1` sheet nori, toasted over open flame 3 sec/side, crumbled to coarse flakes
- [ ] `1 tbsp` white sesame seeds, toasted in dry pan 60 sec until fragrant
- [ ] `½ tsp` **dried chanterelle mushroom powder** (resolved from "chanterelle powder [TO VERIFY]" — grind dried chanterelles to fine powder in spice grinder; sub: ¼ tsp MSG + ¼ tsp dried porcini powder for similar umami)
- [ ] `1 tsp` micro cilantro or shiso leaves (optional, for color)

---

## Instructions

1. **Juice the glaze base.** Feed carrots and grated ginger through a juicer; collect ~⅔ cup (160 ml) bright orange juice. *No juicer? High-speed blend carrots + ginger + 2 tbsp water 60 sec; strain through fine-mesh sieve pressing with spatula — yields ~½ cup. Discard solids.*
2. **Reduce to glaze.** Pour carrot-ginger juice into a small saucepan. Add sugar, both vinegars, and salt. Bring to a simmer over medium-high; cook 6–8 minutes, swirling occasionally, until reduced to ~⅓ cup (80 ml) and thick enough to coat the back of a spoon (220°F / 104°C). *Sensory: syrup clings, bubbles slow and large, aroma shifts from raw carrot to sweet caramelized ginger.* Remove from heat; whisk in sesame oil. Strain through fine sieve into a heatproof jar. Cool to room temp (glaze thickens further).
3. **Preheat the grill.** Heat gas grill to medium-high (450–500°F / 230–260°C) or prepare charcoal until coals are ashed over with a hand-held 3-sec tolerance at grate height. Clean grates; oil lightly with oil-dipped paper towel held in tongs.
4. **Season radicchio.** Toss radicchio wedges in a bowl with olive oil, flaky salt, and pepper until evenly coated. *Key: oil must be thin — excess causes flare-ups.*
5. **Grill cut-side down.** Place wedges cut-side down on hot grill. Cook 3–4 minutes without moving until deep grill marks form and outer leaves char at edges. *Sensory: smoke smells sweet-bitter, leaves curl slightly, core yields to gentle tong pressure.*
6. **Flip and finish.** Flip wedges; grill round-side down 2 minutes just to warm through — do not char the back. Transfer to a wire rack; rest 1 minute (carryover softens the core).
7. **Plate.** Arrange 2 wedges per plate, leaning against each other cut-side up. Spoon 1 tbsp warm glaze over each wedge (or drizzle artistically). *Glaze should pool slightly in the leaf cups.*
8. **Garnish and serve.** Dust lightly with chanterelle powder over the glaze (pinch per wedge). Scatter toasted nori flakes and sesame seeds. Add micro herbs if using. Serve immediately while radicchio is warm and glaze is fluid.

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- **Chanterelle powder resolution:** The notebook's "chanterelle powder [TO VERIFY]" is interpreted as dried chanterelle mushrooms ground to a fine powder — a modern umami seasoning used in Nordic and Japanese-influenced kitchens. It adds earthy, fruity mushroom depth without moisture. If unavailable, a blend of ½ tsp MSG + ½ tsp dried porcini powder mimics the savory boost. Do not omit — the umami dust is what makes the sweet-bitter-salty triangle complete.
- **Juicer vs. blender:** A juicer yields cleaner, more vibrant glaze (pure carrot essence). Blender + strain works but produces a slightly cloudier, more fibrous glaze. If blending, add the 2 tbsp water to get the blades moving.
- **Don't over-reduce:** The glaze continues thickening off heat. Stop at ⅓ cup / 220°F — past that it becomes candy-hard when cooled. If too thick, whisk in 1 tsp hot water at a time.
- **Radicchio variety:** Chioggia (round, red-white) is standard. Treviso (elongated) works — cut lengthwise into 4 spears per head. Grill time drops to 2 min/side.
- **Grill management:** Oil the *food*, not the grates (prevents polymerized oil buildup). If flare-ups char leaves black instead of bronze, move to cooler zone. Goal: caramelized edges, intact structure.

### Variations
- **Miso-Caramel Glaze:** Whisk 1 tbsp white miso into warm glaze (omit salt). Adds fermented depth; pairs with nori beautifully.
- **Blood Orange-Carrot:** Replace half the carrot juice with fresh blood orange juice; omit white wine vinegar. Bright, citrusy, stunning color.
- **Vegan "Bonito" Dust:** Substitute chanterelle powder with ½ tsp toasted nori powder + ¼ tsp shiitake powder + pinch kombu powder — pure plant umami.
- **Grilled Endive Substitute:** Use 4 Belgian endives, halved lengthwise. Milder bitterness, same method.

### Make-Ahead / Storage
- **Carrot-ginger glaze:** Makes ~⅓ cup. Stores 5 days refrigerated in sealed jar; reheat gently (microwave 10 sec) to pourable consistency. Freezes 1 month (ice cube tray, 1 tbsp cubes).
- **Chanterelle powder:** Stores 6 months in airtight jar in freezer (preserves volatile aromatics).
- **Toasted nori / sesame:** Day-of only — both soften within hours.
- **Radicchio:** Cut wedges max 2 hours ahead; hold at room temp covered with damp towel. Cold radicchio shocks on grill and cooks unevenly.
- **Full assembly:** Grill and glaze à la minute. The contrast of warm bitter leaves + room-temp sweet glaze + cool umami dust is the dish.

### Scaling
- **2 servings:** 1 head radicchio (4 wedges), ½ glaze recipe, halve garnish.
- **8 servings:** 4 heads radicchio, 1.5× glaze recipe (evaporation loss scales non-linearly), double garnish. Grill in 2 batches.
- **Restaurant service (24+):** Make glaze morning-of; hold warm in squeeze bottle over bain-marie. Pre-toast nori/sesame. Grill radicchio to order (3 min). Dust chanterelle powder from pinch jar at pass.
