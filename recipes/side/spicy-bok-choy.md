# Spicy Bok Choy

> [!NOTE] Origin
> Best-effort transcription from notebook page 18. Quick high-heat wok dish with caramelized bok choy, garlic, chili, and a soy-sesame finish.

---

## Ingredients

*Base servings: 4 portions*

- [ ] `2 bunches (about 1.5 lbs)` — bok choy, trimmed and halved lengthwise (or quartered if large)
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — neutral oil (peanut or canola for high heat)
- [ ] `2 cloves` — garlic, thinly sliced
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — chili flakes
- [ ] `1 tablespoon` — soy sauce
- [ ] `1 teaspoon` — toasted sesame oil
- [ ] `2 tablespoons` — water (for steaming)

---

## Instructions

1. **Heat the Wok.** Place a wok or large skillet over high heat until a drop of water sizzles and evaporates instantly (~2 min). Add 1 tablespoon oil and swirl to coat; the oil should shimmer but not smoke.

2. **Sear the Bok Choy.** Arrange the bok choy halves cut-side down in a single layer (work in batches if needed). Let sit undisturbed until the cut surfaces are deep golden brown and release easily from the pan (~1–2 min). You should hear a steady sizzle and see a caramelized crust forming.

3. **Add Aromatics.** Flip the bok choy to cook the leaves side briefly (~30 sec). Push the bok choy to the sides of the wok and add the sliced garlic and chili flakes to the center. Stir-fry the aromatics until fragrant and the garlic is just beginning to turn golden at the edges (~15–20 sec) — do not let it brown.

4. **Introduce the Sauce.** Drizzle 1 tablespoon soy sauce over the vegetables and toss everything together to coat. The liquid should sizzle and evaporate quickly, leaving the bok choy glossy.

5. **Steam to Tender-Crisp.** Immediately add 2 tablespoons of water to the wok, then cover tightly with a lid or foil. Let steam until the stems are just tender when pierced with a knife but the leaves remain vibrant green and slightly crisp (~1–2 min).

6. **Finish with Sesame Oil.** Remove the lid and drizzle 1 teaspoon sesame oil over the bok choy. Toss once to distribute, then transfer to a serving plate. The dish should look glistening and smell aromatic.

7. **Serve Immediately.** Serve hot, optionally garnished with toasted sesame seeds or sliced scallions.

---

## Notes & Variations

### Cook's Notes
- Starting with the bok choy cut-side down ensures maximum caramelization on the thick stems, which take longer to cook than the leaves.
- The quick steam step at the end finishes cooking the stems without overcooking the leaves, preserving texture and vibrant green color.

### Variations
- **Garlic-Ginger Bok Choy:** Add 1 teaspoon freshly grated ginger with the garlic for a brighter, more aromatic profile.

### Make-Ahead
- Bok choy is best served immediately after cooking.
- If needed, blanched and shocked bok choy can be prepared ahead: boil halves for 1 minute, shock in ice water, drain, and store dry in the refrigerator for up to 1 day; then finish with the hot wok method just before serving.

### Storage
- Best eaten immediately; leftovers can be refrigerated for up to 1 day and gently reheated, though the texture will soften.

### Scaling
- The recipe scales linearly; use a wok large enough to avoid overcrowding, or cook in batches.
- Increase oil proportionally to maintain a thin coating on the pan surface.
